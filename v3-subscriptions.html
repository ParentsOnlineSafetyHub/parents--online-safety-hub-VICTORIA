Why this matters

Subscription creep is real. A few “small” subscriptions can become $50–$200/month without noticing. This page helps you find them fast and cancel what you don’t use.

Important: Many people have more than one Google/Apple/Microsoft account. To fully clean up subscriptions, you must check every account you’ve used on that device.

Before you start (2-minute checklist)

✅ Make a quick list of emails you’ve used for apps (old + new).
✅ Check subscriptions on each account.
✅ After cancelling, look for a “renews on” date — some stay active until the end of the billing period.

Google Play (Android) — Step by step

Cancel subscriptions
1) Open Google Play Store
2) Tap your profile icon (top right)
3) Tap Payments & subscriptions
4) Tap Subscriptions
5) Select a subscription → Cancel subscription

Check all Google accounts
In Play Store, tap profile icon → tap the email → switch accounts → repeat the steps above.

Also check: Payments
Profile icon → Payments & subscriptions → Budget & history (spot unknown charges).

Apple (iPhone/iPad) — Step by step

Cancel subscriptions
1) Open Settings
2) Tap your Apple ID name at the top
3) Tap Subscriptions
4) Choose a subscription → Cancel Subscription

Check other Apple IDs
If you’ve ever used a different Apple ID: Settings → Apple ID → scroll down → Sign Out (only if you know your login), then sign in to the other Apple ID and re-check Subscriptions.

Family Sharing note: some subscriptions are under a family organiser’s account. Ask the organiser to check their Subscriptions too.

Microsoft — Step by step

Microsoft account subscriptions
1) Go to your Microsoft account (Services & subscriptions)
2) Check recurring billing
3) Turn off Recurring billing or cancel subscription

Also check: Xbox subscriptions
Game Pass / Xbox Live / add-ons can renew automatically depending on account settings.

Check multiple Microsoft accounts
Many families have separate logins for Windows, Xbox, and Outlook. Repeat for each one.

What to do if a charge still happens

1) Re-check the subscription list (wrong account is common)
2) Check email receipts (search: “receipt”, “subscription”, “renewal”)
3) If it’s a card charge: contact your bank and ask about recurring debits
