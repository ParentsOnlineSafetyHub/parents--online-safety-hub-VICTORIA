What is executive functioning?

Executive functioning is the brain’s ability to manage thoughts, emotions, and actions.

  • Stopping before reacting
  • Managing emotions under pressure
  • Thinking about consequences
  • Making better decisions
  • Changing thinking when something feels off
It is the difference between reacting fast and thinking first.

Why it matters for online safety

Most risks happen quickly

Most pressure feels emotional

Most mistakes happen without thinking

Executive functioning slows the moment down

The 5 core safety skills

Impulse Control: pause before acting

Emotional Regulation: stay calm under pressure

Flexible Thinking: consider other possibilities

Critical Thinking: question intent

Decision Making: choose safer outcomes

Why children struggle with this

  • Apps are designed to keep them reacting fast
  • Rewards and attention trigger instant responses
  • Peer pressure increases emotional decisions
  • Embarrassment can override logic
  • They are still developing these skills
Children are not failing — their brain is still developing.

Where executive functioning is most needed

  • Private messages
  • Friend requests
  • Gifts or rewards
  • Requests for photos or personal info
  • Arguments or emotional conflict
  • Embarrassing or risky situations
These are the moments where pause matters most.

The biggest mistake parents make

Only focusing on rules

Not teaching thinking skills

Expecting good decisions without practice

Rules help — but thinking skills protect long term.

How to teach this properly

  • Teach one skill at a time
  • Use real-life examples
  • Repeat often
  • Keep language simple
  • Practise “what would you do?” scenarios
Children learn thinking by practising it — not just hearing it.
