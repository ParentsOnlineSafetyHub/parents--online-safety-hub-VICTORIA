Why parents miss risk on PlayStation

Parents often think the main risk is the game itself.

But messages, parties, headsets, friend requests, and voice chat are often where the contact becomes more personal and more private.

The biggest risks usually appear in the spaces wrapped around the game

Platform setup (quick steps)

  • Go to Settings → Users & Accounts → Privacy.
  • Set messages to Friends Only where possible.
  • Restrict voice chat and party chat.
  • Use Family & Parental Controls for spending limits and approvals.
  • Review the friends list regularly.
  • Check who your child is talking to, not just what they are playing.
If menus differ, use the Settings search and type: privacy, family, or messages.

What parents should lock down first

  • Messages and friend requests
  • Party chat and voice communication
  • Purchases and wallet access
  • New contacts and who can see the account
  • Game-specific communication settings
  • How often the child is joining private groups or parties
Safer platform settings reduce exposure, but they do not replace awareness.
