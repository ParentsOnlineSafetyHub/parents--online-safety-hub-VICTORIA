In-Game Currency & Grooming Risk

“Free Robux” is not generosity.
It is often the first step in manipulation.

Where grooming often begins
GIFTS → TRUST → SECRETS → CONTROL
Many grooming situations do not start with threats.
They start with something that feels positive — rewards, gifts, or help inside a game.
Predators often “invest” in children first
before they ever ask for anything back.

What this looks like in real life

  • “I’ll send you Robux”
  • “I’ll gift you a skin”
  • “I can help you level up faster”
  • “I’ll buy you stuff if you trust me”
  • “Don’t tell anyone, this is just between us”
At first, it feels like kindness.
But it is often the start of control.

Why this works on kids

  • Kids value in-game currency as real reward
  • It creates fast trust with strangers
  • It builds emotional attachment
  • It creates a sense of “owing something back”
  • It isolates the child through secrecy
This is not random behaviour.
This is a known grooming pattern.

How it escalates

Gift / Robux / Skin
↓
Friendly chats
↓
Private messaging
↓
Secrets
↓
Control / manipulation
Once the child feels attached, the direction can shift fast.

What this can turn into

Grooming

Sextortion

Blackmail

Emotional control

Isolation from parents

The gift is not the end goal. It is the entry point.

What parents should do

  • Do not allow private gifting without approval
  • Ask: “Who gave you that?”
  • Check friend lists and recent interactions
  • Watch for secrecy around rewards or currency
  • Set clear rules about accepting gifts from others
If a child receives gifts and becomes secretive, act early.

What needs to change

Children should not be able to receive digital gifts from strangers without parental approval.
  • Parental approval for all incoming gifts
  • Visibility on who sent items
  • Alerts for repeated gifting
  • Block unknown senders by default
If gifting is a known grooming tool, platforms should not leave it unprotected.
