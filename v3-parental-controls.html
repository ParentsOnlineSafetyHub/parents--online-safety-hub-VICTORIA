Parental Controls

Parental controls help — but they are not the full solution.
They reduce risk. They do not replace awareness, conversation, or involvement.

Start here:
Parental controls are not about spying.
They are about visibility, boundaries, and reducing hidden risk.

The reality

Parental controls can block, limit, and monitor activity

But they cannot fully stop risk, secrecy, or manipulation

Controls reduce risk — they do not replace parenting

What parental controls actually do

  • Limit screen time
  • Block or restrict apps
  • Control content access
  • Manage downloads and purchases
  • Limit who can contact your child
  • Show activity or usage patterns
They are strongest when used before problems start — not after.

What parental controls do NOT do

  • They do not stop a child from hiding things
  • They do not replace conversations
  • They do not prevent all contact with strangers
  • They do not stop manipulation once trust is built
  • They do not guarantee safety on their own
If a child feels they must hide something, controls alone will not fix that.

Biggest mistake parents make

Setting controls once and never checking again

Assuming controls = safety

Not understanding how apps actually work

Ignoring behaviour changes because “controls are on”

Controls without awareness create a false sense of security.

What actually works best

  • Controls + conversation
  • Controls + house rules
  • Controls + device visibility
  • Controls + regular check-ins
  • Controls + understanding the platforms your child uses
The safest setup is not one tool — it is a system.
