Platforms

Lock the platform first. Then lock the game or app running on it.

Why platforms matter

Platforms control communication, friend requests, purchases, invites, and multiplayer access.

These settings are often stronger than the settings inside the game itself.

Platform first. Then app and game safety.

What parents should understand

  • Many online risks begin through platform messaging, party systems, or friend requests before parents even look at the game.
  • A platform can control who contacts a child, who sends invites, and who can join private or multiplayer activity.
  • If the platform is left open, every game on it becomes harder to manage safely.
Parents often focus on the game first, but the platform is usually the stronger safety layer.

What to lock on every platform

1) Messages and private communication

2) Friend requests and invites

3) Voice chat and multiplayer exposure

4) Purchases and spending

5) Who can join or contact the child

If a platform lets strangers message, invite, or talk to your child freely, the risk is already too high.

Best order for parents

The safest path is usually this:

Lock the platform
↓
Check the game or app
↓
Watch for off-platform movement
↓
Act early if something feels wrong

Quick reminder for parents

Games and apps can change, but the safety principles stay the same.
