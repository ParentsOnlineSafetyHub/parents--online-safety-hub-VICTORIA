Platforms

Every device is a gateway.
Lock the platform first. Then control what runs on it.

Platform = control layer
PLATFORMS CONTROL WHO CAN REACH YOUR CHILD
Platforms manage messaging, party chat, friend requests, purchases, and multiplayer access. If the platform is open, every game and app becomes harder to control safely.
Parents often start with the game.
The strongest protection usually starts at the platform level.

Why platforms matter

Platforms control who can contact your child.

They control how communication begins and how quickly it escalates.

If the platform is open, the risk is already active

What parents should understand

  • Most contact begins through platform messaging or invites
  • Party systems and chat features create fast familiarity
  • Games often rely on the platform’s communication system
  • Stronger platform settings reduce risk across every game
The platform is the control layer. The game is just the environment.

Choose your platform

PC PlayStation Xbox Nintendo Switch Virtual Reality
Each platform has different risks — but the same safety principles apply.

What to lock on every platform

  • Messages and private communication
  • Friend requests and invites
  • Voice chat and multiplayer exposure
  • Purchases and spending
  • Who can contact or join your child
If strangers can freely message or invite your child, the setup is too open.

The safest order

Parents get the best results when they follow this order:

Lock the platform
↓
Check the game or app
↓
Watch communication patterns
↓
Notice private movement
↓
Act early if something feels off
