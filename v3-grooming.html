Grooming Signs & What To Do

Clarity beats panic. Know the pattern. Act early.

What grooming looks like

Grooming is a process. It often starts harmless — then slowly shifts into secrecy, sexual topics, manipulation, and isolation from parents.

  • Trust building: “Best friend” behaviour, constant contact, gifts/items.
  • Isolation: moving to private apps, private calls, private servers/worlds.
  • Secrecy: “Don’t tell your parents.”
  • Desensitising: sexual jokes → explicit content → requests.
  • Control: threats, guilt, fear, “you’ll get in trouble too.”

Non-Negotiable

Kids do NOT get punished for telling the truth.

If they fear losing devices, they hide problems. Your calm response is protection.

Warning signs in a child

  • Sudden secrecy, hiding screens, clearing chats/history.
  • Big mood change after using apps (angry, sad, anxious).
  • New “online friend” they won’t explain.
  • Using devices late at night or refusing device checks.
  • Talk of gifts, Robux, skins, money, “someone is helping me.”

What parents should do (step-by-step)

  • 1) Stay calm. Say: “You’re not in trouble.”
  • 2) Screenshot evidence (messages, usernames, server names).
  • 3) Block/report inside the app.
  • 4) Lock down device + account settings (DMs, chat, friend requests).
  • 5) Use reporting pathways when needed.
Reporting (Australia) Device Controls

Simple script (use this)

“I’m not here to take your phone. I’m here to protect you. If someone online makes you uncomfortable, you tell me and we handle it together.”

Next action
