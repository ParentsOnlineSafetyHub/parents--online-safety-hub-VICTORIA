Report & Get Help

If something feels wrong, act early. Save evidence. Report. Protect your child.

Act early, stay calm

If a child is in immediate danger, call emergency services straight away.

If something harmful has already happened online, preserve evidence first, then report through the platform and the right official pathway.

Calm action protects children faster than panic

Emergency vs non-emergency

Emergency / immediate danger: Call Triple Zero (000)

Non-urgent police assistance: Police Assistance Line 131 444

Anonymous tips: Crime Stoppers 1800 333 000

If a child is in immediate danger, do not wait to gather everything perfectly. Get urgent help first.

Report harmful or illegal online content

The Australian eSafety Commissioner can help with seriously harmful online content, including illegal and restricted content, image-based abuse, and other serious online harm.

Report Online Harm (eSafety) eSafety Report Forms

Also report directly inside the platform or app using Report and Block.

Australian Centre to Counter Child Exploitation

The ACCCE provides a reporting pathway for online child sexual exploitation concerns.

Report Online Child Sexual Exploitation (ACCCE)
If the concern involves online sexual exploitation of a child, this is one of the most important reporting pathways in Australia.

Sextortion or blackmail

If someone is threatening to release images, demanding money, or pressuring a child to send more material, stop communication and preserve evidence immediately.

Do not send money.
Do not send more images.
Save evidence and report immediately.
Sextortion Guide Report to eSafety

Evidence checklist

1) Screenshot messages with dates and times.

2) Save usernames, profile links, and gamer tags.

3) Record the platform, group, server, or world name.

4) Preserve evidence before blocking where safe to do so.

5) Do not warn the offender before reporting.

Evidence helps platforms, eSafety, and police understand what happened and act faster.

Report directly on platforms

Reporting inside platforms helps remove offenders and protect other children using the same space.
