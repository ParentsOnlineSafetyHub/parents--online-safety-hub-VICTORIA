Google Meet Safety Guide

Links can be forwarded. Use trusted accounts and controlled entry.

Why Google Meet Needs Supervision

Google Meet is common for schools and families. The main risks are meeting links being forwarded, unknown participants entering, and screen sharing/chat being misused.

Step-by-Step Safety Setup

1) Prefer school-managed Google accounts

If your child uses Meet for school, use the school account — these often have safer controls.

2) Join meetings only from trusted sources

Rules: • Only join links from school email or a known parent/guardian • Never join random links from games/social apps

3) Use fresh links for private calls

Avoid reusing old links. If a link is reused, it can be shared without permission.

4) Screen sharing

If available in settings: • Restrict who can share • Supervise if screen sharing is used

5) Supervise younger children

Calls should be in shared spaces for younger kids. No private bedroom calls.

House Rule

If a child is told “don’t tell your parents” — that is a red flag. End the call and report.
