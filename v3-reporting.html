Step 1 — Protect the child first

Stay calm, keep the child close, reduce further contact where possible, and do not make them feel blamed.

The first job is safety, not interrogation.

Step 2 — Preserve the evidence

Before deleting, blocking, or confronting anyone, save the information that may later matter.

Screenshots of chats and profiles

Usernames, display names, links, dates, and times

Threats, sexual requests, secrecy, blackmail, or pressure

Anything showing who, where, what, and when

Once evidence is gone, you may not get another chance to save it.

What to do immediately

  • Stay calm and keep your child close
  • Tell them they are not in trouble
  • Stop further contact if safe
  • Take screenshots of everything
  • Save usernames, profiles, and timestamps
  • Write down what happened
  • Do not delete evidence
Children speak more openly when they feel safe, not blamed.
How to Save Evidence Properly Parent Scripts First 24 Hours Check a Device Calmly

What not to do

  • Do not confront the person directly
  • Do not shame or punish your child
  • Do not delete chats or accounts too early
  • Do not post accusations publicly
  • Do not assume blocking alone solves everything
Rushed reactions can destroy evidence or stop your child from opening up.

How to talk to your child

  • Stay calm and simple
  • “You are not in trouble”
  • “I’m glad you told me”
  • “Can you show me?”
  • Avoid pressure or interrogation
Belief builds trust. Blame shuts it down.
