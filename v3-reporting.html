Report & Get Help

If something feels wrong, act early. Save evidence. Report. Protect your child.

Child Safety First: Reporting matters, but so does the bigger picture — reducing exposure, spotting warning signs early, and helping children feel safe enough to speak up.

Most important rule

If your child has been targeted, your first job is to stay calm.

Calm parents get more truth, preserve more evidence, and protect their child better.

Stay calm. Save evidence. Act early.

Emergency vs non-emergency

Emergency / immediate danger: Call Triple Zero (000)

Non-urgent police assistance: Police Assistance Line 131 444

Anonymous tips: Crime Stoppers 1800 333 000

Do this first

1) Stay calm and reassure your child they are not in trouble

2) Screenshot messages, usernames, dates, times, and profile links

3) Do not argue with or warn the offender

4) Report inside the platform/app

5) Report to the right authority

Say this to your child:
“Thank you for telling me. You are not in trouble. We are going to handle this together.”

Report harmful or illegal online content

The Australian eSafety Commissioner can help with seriously harmful online content including child sexual abuse material, abuse, and exploitation.

Sextortion or blackmail

If someone is threatening to release images or demanding money, stop communication and save evidence.

Do not send money.
Do not send more images.
Save evidence and report immediately.
