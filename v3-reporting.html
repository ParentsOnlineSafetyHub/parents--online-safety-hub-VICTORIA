Reporting

If something feels wrong, act early. Save evidence. Report. Protect your child.

Emergency vs non-emergency

Emergency / immediate danger: Call Triple Zero (000)

Non-urgent police assistance: Police Assistance Line 131 444 (connects you to your local police station)

Anonymous tips: Crime Stoppers 1800 333 000

Report harmful or illegal online content

The Australian eSafety Commissioner can help remove seriously harmful online content including harassment, image-based abuse, and child sexual abuse material.

Report Online Harm (eSafety) eSafety Report Forms

Also report directly inside the platform or app using the Report or Block tools.

Australian Centre to Counter Child Exploitation

The ACCCE investigates online child exploitation and works with Australian police. Use their portal to report child sexual abuse material or online exploitation.

Report Child Exploitation (ACCCE)

Sextortion or Blackmail

Sextortion happens when someone threatens to release images or private information unless the victim sends more images or money.

Do not send money.
Do not send more images.
Save evidence and report immediately.
Sextortion Guide Report to eSafety

Evidence checklist

1) Screenshot messages with dates and times.

2) Save usernames and profile links.

3) Record the platform, group, or server name.

4) Do not warn the offender before reporting.

5) Preserve evidence before blocking.

Report directly on platforms

Reporting inside platforms helps remove offenders and protect other children.
