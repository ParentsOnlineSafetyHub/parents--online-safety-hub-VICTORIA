YouTube

YouTube is not just videos. It is also comments, recommendations, live chat, and algorithm exposure.

Why YouTube matters

Parents often think YouTube is passive watching, but the risks come through comments, livestream chat, algorithm recommendations, and harmful content exposure.

It can influence children long before a parent realises what is appearing in the feed.

What a child watches changes what YouTube shows next

Important YouTube settings

1) Use supervised accounts or YouTube Kids where appropriate

2) Turn on restricted mode where helpful

3) Review watch history and subscriptions

4) Avoid comment sections and live chats for younger users

5) Watch for algorithm shifts into unsafe content

Major red flags on YouTube

  • Sexualised or violent recommended content
  • Livestream chat exposing the child to strangers
  • Repeated exposure to gore, shock, or manipulative content
  • Creators or commenters directing children to other apps
  • Children hiding watch history or subscriptions
