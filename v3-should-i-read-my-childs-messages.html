Should I Read My Child’s Messages?

This is one of the hardest questions parents face. It is not about control — it is about safety.

The real answer

If there is a safety concern — yes.

If everything is stable — use open communication first.

Safety comes before privacy when risk is involved

When you SHOULD check

  • Behaviour changes suddenly
  • Secrecy increases
  • Unknown contacts appear
  • Emotional withdrawal or stress
  • Warnings from others

When to pause first

  • No signs of risk
  • Open communication exists
  • Trust is strong and consistent
Start with conversation. Move to checking if needed.

How to do it properly

Stay calm

Explain why you are checking

Do not ambush

Look before reacting

Focus on safety, not punishment
