Safety Awareness

Know the playbook. Predators use patterns. Parents can break them.

This is happening right now

Online predators actively target children across gaming and social platforms.

They rarely start obvious. They build trust, isolate the child, and then escalate.

The earlier a parent acts, the safer the child is

Predator tactics breakdown

1

Target & Access

Open games, public servers, group chats, comments, livestreams, and “kid friendly” spaces.

↓
2

Trust Building

Friendly tone, shared interests, compliments, attention, gifts, and flattery.

↓
3

Isolation

Moving from public spaces into private messages, voice chat, or smaller groups.

↓
4

Secrecy & Control

“Don’t tell your parents.” “They won’t understand.” “You’ll get in trouble.”

↓
5

Exploitation

Sexual content, manipulation, blackmail, threats, shame, or extortion.

Why kids don’t tell parents

  • They fear losing their device
  • They feel embarrassed or ashamed
  • They think they caused the problem
  • They have been told they will get in trouble
  • They have been pressured into secrecy
Most important message: “You will not be in trouble for telling me.”
