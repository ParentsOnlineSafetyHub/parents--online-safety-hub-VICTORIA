Why Fortnite Is High Exposure

Fortnite is not unsafe by default. It becomes high exposure due to:

• Massive global player base • Open voice chat by default • Cross-platform play (mobile, console, PC) • Public matchmaking with strangers • Competitive culture & peer pressure • Live events & community interactions

High player volume + open voice chat = higher contact risk.

The Real Issue

Most parents assume: • “It’s just a shooter game.” • “They’re only playing with friends.” • “Voice chat isn’t that important.”

But open voice chat allows strangers direct access. Default settings are rarely safest settings.

Step-By-Step: Reduce Fortnite Risk

1) Turn Off Voice Chat
Settings → Audio → Voice Chat → Off (or Friends Only)

2) Restrict Text Chat
Settings → Account & Privacy → Social Privacy → Friends Only

3) Disable Friend Requests
Set to “Friends of Friends” or restrict completely.

4) Enable Parental Controls
Epic Account → Parental Controls → Set PIN

5) Use Platform-Level Controls
Apply Xbox / PlayStation / Switch restrictions first.

6) Review Recent Players
Remove unknown contacts weekly.
