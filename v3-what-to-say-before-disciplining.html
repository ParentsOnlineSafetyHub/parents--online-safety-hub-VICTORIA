What to Say Before Disciplining

The first words shape the entire outcome.
Start wrong, and the conversation shuts down before it begins.

The moment that matters most

Your tone sets the direction

Your words control openness

Your reaction shapes honesty

If a child feels attacked, they stop telling the truth.

What NOT to say first

  • “What were you thinking?”
  • “You know better than that”
  • “Why would you do that?”

What to say instead

“You’re not in trouble — I just want to understand”

“Show me what happened”

“Let’s go through this together”

This keeps the child in communication mode, not defence mode.

Why this matters online

  • Kids hide mistakes when they fear punishment
  • Online risks grow when parents lose visibility
  • Early honesty prevents escalation
