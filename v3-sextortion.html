Sextortion

Act fast. Stay calm. Sextortion relies on fear, shame, and panic. Parents need a clear response plan.

Most important rule

If someone is threatening your child with images, screenshots, or blackmail, do not panic.

Calm action protects better than emotional reaction.

Do not send money. Do not send more images.

What sextortion looks like

  • Threats to send images to family or friends
  • Demands for money, gift cards, or cryptocurrency
  • Pressure to send more images or videos
  • Claims that the victim “started it” or will be blamed

What to do right now

1) Reassure your child they are not in trouble

2) Stop communication with the offender

3) Screenshot everything: usernames, threats, dates, payment requests

4) Report to eSafety and police if needed

5) Do not negotiate, pay, or send more content
