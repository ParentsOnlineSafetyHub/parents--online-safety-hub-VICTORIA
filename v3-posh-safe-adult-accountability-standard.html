Official POSH Standard Proposal

POSH Safe Adult Accountability Standard

A proposed voluntary child-safety accountability standard for parents, carers, sitters, teachers, coaches, clubs, organisations, family networks, and safe adults who want clearer boundaries before children are placed in trusted environments.

Important: POSH does not replace Working With Children Checks, police checks, official screening, child protection, courts, police, safeguarding policies, or legal reporting pathways. This standard is an extra accountability layer, not proof that any person is safe or unsafe.

The standard in one sentence

The POSH Safe Adult Accountability Standard is a voluntary system where adults agree to clear child-safety boundaries, accept written accountability, receive status-only verification, and understand that breaches, false declarations, or malicious misuse can result in removal and referral to the appropriate authorities.

What POSH can and cannot say

POSH can say

This adult currently holds active POSH accountability status and has voluntarily accepted the POSH Safe Adult Accountability Standard.

POSH cannot say

This adult is guaranteed safe, risk-free, legally cleared, or safer than someone who has not joined.

POSH can record

The adult accepted boundaries around secrecy, private contact, digital communication, grooming behaviour, reporting, false information, and misuse.

POSH cannot replace

Working With Children Checks, police checks, child protection, safeguarding authorities, courts, police, employers, schools, or legal investigation.

The 7 core rules of the POSH standard

These rules are the heart of the standard. They keep the system focused on child safety, accountability, transparency, and preventing misuse.

1. No secrecy with children

A safe adult must not ask, pressure, reward, manipulate, threaten, or encourage a child to keep secrets from parents, carers, guardians, teachers, police, child protection, or another safe adult.

2. No hidden private contact

A safe adult must not create hidden private contact with a child through messages, apps, games, social media, voice chat, disappearing messages, secret accounts, gifts, or off-platform movement.

3. No grooming behaviour

A safe adult must not use gifts, attention, emotional dependency, isolation, secrecy, pressure, shame, threats, blackmail, sexualised comments, or hidden access to manipulate a child.

4. No misuse of trust or access

A trusted role must never be used to gain unsafe access to a child. This includes family trust, coaching, teaching, mentoring, babysitting, transport, online contact, or community roles.

5. Child-safety concern pathway

A safe adult agrees to act when a child may be unsafe by using the appropriate parent, carer, safeguarding lead, child protection service, police, or relevant authority.

6. No false or misleading information

A member must not provide false, misleading, hidden, manipulated, or dishonest information during application, renewal, verification, reporting, or review.

7. No malicious misuse of the system

A member must not use POSH to make knowingly false accusations, manipulate disputes, target another person, damage reputations, harass, retaliate, or push a personal agenda.

The balance

POSH must protect genuine child-safety reporting while also preventing people from weaponising the system with false or malicious claims.

Verification should use POSH ID or QR code

Status only

The system should not show private details, allegations, reports, criminal history, or sensitive information.

Consent-based checking

The adult shares their ID or QR code when they want to prove they participate in the POSH accountability system.

Status language must stay neutral

Active

This person currently holds active POSH Safe Adult Accountability status.

Expired

This person previously held POSH status, but it has expired and has not been renewed.

Suspended pending review

This status is temporarily unavailable while POSH reviews information under its safeguarding process.

No active status

POSH cannot confirm active accountability status for this ID or QR code.

POSH should avoid public labels such as unsafe, dangerous, offender, banned, predator, guilty, accused, flagged, exposed, or suspicious.

The $5 one-time contribution model

The proposed contribution should be low, transparent, and tied to system maintenance — not profit, punishment, or access control.

Low barrier

A one-time $5 contribution keeps the system accessible while still helping cover secure storage, document handling, private administration, verification tools, and maintenance.

Fee waiver option

POSH should allow fee waivers where cost would prevent a genuine safe adult from participating.

Long-term position

POSH’s broader position should remain that core child-safety checks should be free or government-funded so cost never blocks safeguarding.

If a member is removed for a confirmed breach, POSH may refund the contribution while removing active status. This helps show the fee is for secure system maintenance, not a punishment mechanism.

False or misleading information clause

This clause protects the system from manipulators, false claims, dishonest declarations, and personal-agenda misuse while still protecting genuine child-safety reporting.

Draft clause

I agree that all information I provide to POSH must be true, accurate, complete, and not misleading to the best of my knowledge.

I understand that knowingly providing false, misleading, manipulated, incomplete, or dishonest information may result in immediate suspension, removal from active POSH status, cancellation of my membership, and referral of relevant information to the appropriate authorities where lawful and necessary.

I understand that POSH must protect genuine child-safety reports made in good faith. However, I must not use POSH to knowingly make false accusations, manipulate disputes, target another person, harass, retaliate, damage reputations, or misuse the system for a personal agenda.

I understand that misuse of a child-safety system is itself unsafe conduct because it weakens trust, distracts from genuine risk, and can cause harm to children, families, safe adults, and proper safeguarding processes.

Concern and breach pathway

POSH must never act like a court. It should preserve information, protect children, avoid public accusation, and direct serious matters to proper authorities.

Fairness rule: a report does not automatically mean guilt.

POSH must separate urgent child protection from public accusation. Children must be protected, but adults must not be publicly labelled without lawful process.

What removal means

Removal does not publicly mean guilty

Removal means the person no longer holds active POSH accountability status. It should not publicly mean offender, criminal, unsafe, guilty, or predator.

Removal protects the standard

If a breach is confirmed, removal protects the integrity of the system and keeps the standard focused on child safety.

Authorities remain the proper pathway

Serious concerns, criminal allegations, child-safety risks, or false declarations should be directed to the proper authorities where lawful and necessary.

Private record, public restraint

POSH can privately preserve signed agreements and review records without creating public accusation lists.

Who the standard is for

Parents & carers

To request clearer accountability before babysitting, sleepovers, transport, coaching, mentoring, online contact, or regular access to children.

Safe adults

To show they accept boundaries, understand grooming risks, and are willing to operate with transparency.

Babysitters & family friends

To show families they accept child-safety boundaries before being trusted with children.

Teachers & coaches

To support professional child-safe behaviour around communication, privacy, trust, secrecy, and reporting.

Clubs & organisations

To add a visible accountability layer beside existing screening, safeguarding, training, and supervision.

Community networks

To help families build safer cultures where adults understand the same rules and children know who they can talk to.

Implementation roadmap

This becomes a standard by building documents first, testing the wording, reviewing risks, then building the application.

Step 1 — Standard page

Publish this page as the official POSH standard proposal so the public can understand the concept before the app exists.

Step 2 — Printable standard PDF

Create a printable version of this page for parents, organisations, clubs, reviewers, and community discussions.

Step 3 — Agreement PDF

Create the signing agreement with identity fields, clauses, declarations, signature, witness, false information clause, and misuse clause.

Step 4 — Review pack

Ask lawyers, safeguarding workers, parents, carers, teachers, coaches, and organisations to review the wording and risks.

Step 5 — Privacy model

Define what is stored, how it is secured, what the QR page shows, how removals work, and how authority referrals are handled.

Step 6 — Pilot program

Test the standard with a small group before allowing wider sign-ups.

Step 7 — Application build

Build adult applications, agreement signing, POSH ID, QR verification, status-only lookup, concern reporting, admin review, and audit trail.

Step 8 — Government proposal

Use pilot data to argue for free or government-funded child-safety checks and broader adoption of accountability standards.

Official POSH position

POSH supports more child-safety checks, free or government-funded access to core screening, stronger adult accountability, clearer safeguarding culture, and better reporting pathways.

POSH does not support public vigilantism, public accusation lists, exposing private registers, or replacing proper legal and safeguarding systems.

The goal is simple: make safe adults easier to identify, make boundaries clearer, make misuse harder, and make unsafe behaviour harder to hide.

Help shape the standard

POSH is seeking feedback on the wording, fairness, privacy risks, legal risks, child-safety usefulness, misuse prevention, and practical application of this proposed standard.
