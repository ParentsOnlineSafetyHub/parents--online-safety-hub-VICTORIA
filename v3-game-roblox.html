Why Roblox Is High Exposure

Roblox is not just a game — it is a massive social platform.

Large numbers of children, chat systems, friend requests and private game worlds create opportunities for strangers to contact kids. Predators often try to move conversations to other apps where moderation is weaker.

  • Chat + DMs allow strangers to initiate conversations
  • User-created games have inconsistent moderation
  • Private servers reduce visibility
  • Virtual currency (Robux) can be used to manipulate children

Top 3 High Exposure Platforms

These platforms appear most frequently in online grooming investigations due to their combination of chat, voice communication and large youth audiences.

  • Roblox — massive child audience + chat + private servers
  • Discord — servers, DMs, voice rooms and private groups
  • VRChat — voice chat, avatars and private worlds

The most common grooming pattern is moving a child from a game to a private messaging platform such as Discord, Snapchat, Telegram or WhatsApp.

Predators & Off-Platform Luring Grooming Warning Signs

Quick win (60 seconds)

If you do nothing else today: lock communication, lock who can add friends, and add a parent PIN.

Step-By-Step (simple clicks)

On the child’s Roblox account:

1) Open Roblox → tap More (⋯) / menu
2) Go to Settings → Privacy
3) Set Who can message me = No one (or Friends only)
4) Set Who can chat with me in-app = No one (or Friends only)
5) Set Who can join me in experiences = Friends
6) Set Who can follow me into experiences = No one / Friends
7) Set Who can add me as a friend = Friends
8) Turn off Camera / Microphone access at device level unless required

Parent controls (recommended):

1) Go to Settings → Parental Controls
2) Turn on Parental Controls
3) Set a Parent PIN
4) Review Spending / Purchases and lock it down

If your child plays on console or PC, apply platform safety settings too: Platforms hub.
