Best Parental Control Apps

No app replaces parenting. But the right tool can give parents more visibility, better control, and earlier warning signs.

Start with the truth

Parental control apps can help with supervision, limits, app approvals, and visibility.

They do not replace calm conversations, house rules, or understanding how risk actually works.

A monitoring app can support safety, but it cannot build trust by itself

Good options parents often use

  • Google Family Link
  • Apple Screen Time
  • Microsoft Family Safety
  • Qustodio
  • Bark
  • Norton Family
Built-in tools are often the best place to start before paying for more advanced monitoring.

What these apps are good for

  • Managing screen time
  • Approving or blocking app installs
  • Viewing device use patterns
  • Setting time limits and downtime
  • Reducing exposure to unsuitable content
  • Helping parents keep structure around device use

What they are not good at

  • Stopping all manipulation or grooming
  • Understanding emotional risk on their own
  • Replacing real conversations
  • Preventing secrecy if the child uses other devices or accounts
  • Making parents automatically aware of the bigger pattern
A strong app can show activity. It cannot always explain what that activity means.

How to choose the right one

Match it to your device type first

Decide whether you need basic controls or deeper monitoring

Keep the setup simple enough that you will actually use it

Focus on consistency, not just features

Helping parents choose better tools can reduce risk earlier.
