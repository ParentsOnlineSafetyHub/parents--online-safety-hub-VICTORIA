10 Signs Your Child May Be Targeted Online

Not every sign means danger. But when multiple signs appear together, parents should pay attention early.

Why this page matters

Online grooming and manipulation usually do not begin with something obvious.

It often starts with secrecy, private contact, gifts, emotional dependence, or sudden changes in behaviour.

Patterns matter more than one single moment

1. Sudden secrecy around devices

Hiding screens, clearing chats, turning devices away, switching apps fast, or refusing normal checks can be warning signs.

2. A new online “friend” they will not explain

If your child talks often to someone online but becomes vague, defensive, or secretive when asked who they are, pay attention.

3. Mood changes after gaming or social apps

Anxiety, anger, shame, sadness, panic, or emotional crashes after being online can signal harmful contact or pressure.

4. Repeated late-night device use

Hidden contact often grows in late hours when children feel more isolated and parents are less likely to notice.

5. Gifts, Robux, skins, money, or “help” from someone online

Gifts can be used to build trust, create obligation, or make a child feel loyal to the person giving them.

6. Pressure to move to another app

If contact moves from a game or public platform into Discord, Snapchat, Telegram, WhatsApp, or private messages, the risk usually increases.

7. Talk of secrecy

“Don’t tell your parents,” “they won’t understand,” or “this is just between us” are major warning signs.

8. Increasing emotional attachment to someone online

If your child seems deeply attached to a person they only know online, especially someone older, that can signal manipulation or dependency.

9. Requests for photos, age, location, or personal details

Any pressure for private details, photos, videos, or more personal contact should be treated seriously.

10. Your instincts say something feels off

Parents do not need perfect proof before acting. If the pattern feels wrong, that is enough reason to slow things down and check.

Something “feeling off” is enough reason to act earlier.

What to do next

If several signs are appearing together, move calmly and clearly.
