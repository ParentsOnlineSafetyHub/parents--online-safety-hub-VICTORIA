Why Roblox Appears at the Top of Exposure Lists

Roblox is not dangerous by design — it is high exposure because of scale and structure.

• Over 70 million daily users • Massive under-13 user base • User-generated games (anyone can create content) • In-game chat (text & voice) • Private servers • Avatar customisation & social interaction • Cross-platform access (mobile, PC, console)

High user volume + open chat systems + child-dominant audience = higher exposure potential.

The Real Issue

The problem isn’t Roblox alone. It’s default settings.

Most parents download the app and assume: • It's just a game • It's cartoon style so it’s safe • Chat won’t be a problem • Their child “would tell them” if something happened

Digital safety fails when settings stay on default.

Step-By-Step: Reducing Roblox Risk

1) Set Account Age Correctly
Log in → Settings → Account Info → Ensure real birth year is entered.

2) Enable Parental Controls
Settings → Parental Controls → Add Parent PIN.

3) Restrict Communication
Settings → Privacy → Communication →
Set: • Who can chat with me? → Friends
• Who can chat with me in app? → Friends
• Who can chat with me in game? → Friends

4) Turn Off Voice Chat (Under 13 Recommended)
Settings → Privacy → Voice Chat → Disable

5) Restrict Private Servers
Limit who can invite your child to private servers.

6) Review Friends List Weekly
Remove unknown or real-world strangers.

7) Use Device Controls First
Always apply Apple Screen Time or Google Family Link before adjusting game settings.

The Bigger Picture

We do not remove children from the digital world. We prepare them for it.

Information is vast. Misinformation is common. Head-in-the-sand parenting no longer works.

Protection is proactive. It is calm. It is consistent. It is shared between parents.
