What POSH is all about

POSH stands for Parents Online Safety Hub.

It exists to help parents, carers, families, and anyone protecting children make better decisions earlier — before confusion becomes harm, and before small warning signs become bigger problems.

POSH is not built around panic. It is built around awareness, action, and child safety. The goal is to help people understand what is happening, recognise real warning signs, reduce exposure, respond calmly, and move toward the right next step.

POSH also helps families understand both online stranger risk and known-person risk, because harm does not always come from someone unknown.

POSH helps families:
spot risk earlier, understand harmful patterns, tighten safety settings, protect trust, and act with more clarity when something feels off.

Child Safety First:
Online risks are not limited to one game or one app. Exposure can happen through gaming, social media, livestreams, private chats, algorithms, digital communities, AI chat tools, behaviour patterns, repeated media exposure, and adults or older peers already known to the child.

Most parents don’t realise how grooming starts

It rarely starts with something obvious.

It usually begins with a simple conversation in a game, a social app, a group chat, or through someone already trusted in real life.

Then the contact often moves into:

Discord Snapchat Telegram Private Messaging Known Adults
That shift into private space or private trust is where manipulation often begins.

Why POSH exists

POSH exists because too many families only learn about online danger after harm has already started.

Children are being pulled into private chats, manipulated through games, exposed to unsafe content, pressured by people they meet online, and sometimes harmed by people already known to them in real life.

This platform was built to help parents act earlier, understand more, and protect with confidence instead of panic.
