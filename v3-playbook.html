Predator Playbook

Predators follow patterns. Understanding the pattern helps you stop it early.

The Grooming Escalation Path

Online grooming usually happens step by step. Predators slowly move children from safe public spaces into private conversations where control increases.

The moment someone asks a child to move to another app, treat it as a warning sign.

Typical Escalation

1

Public Games

Predators often start in open communities with children.

Roblox • Minecraft • Fortnite

↓
2

Private Messages

They move conversations into private messages inside the game.

Direct messages or party chat

↓
3

Discord Servers

Predators invite children to Discord where moderation is weaker.

Private servers • Voice chat

↓
4

Private Apps

Communication moves to platforms with disappearing messages.

Snapchat • Telegram • WhatsApp

↓
5

Exploitation

Manipulation, sextortion, or coercion may begin.

The Critical Warning Sign

If someone asks your child to move chats off the platform they met on — that is a major red flag.

Safe people do not need secret conversations.

Move chat off-platform = grooming escalation

How Parents Break the Pattern

  • Keep game chats restricted to friends
  • Disable direct messages from strangers
  • Teach kids never to move chats to other apps
  • Regularly check friends lists
  • Encourage kids to report uncomfortable conversations
