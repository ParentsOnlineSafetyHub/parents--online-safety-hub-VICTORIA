Main USA reporting pathways

Use the pathway that matches the situation:

  • NCMEC CyberTipline: grooming, exploitation, sexual contact, CSAM, trafficking
  • IC3 (FBI): sextortion scams, fraud, or cyber-enabled crime
  • Police / 911: immediate danger or real-world risk
NCMEC CyberTipline FBI IC3 FBI Cyber Info
If a child is being targeted, groomed, or exploited, NCMEC is the primary reporting path in the USA.

What to do immediately

  • Keep the child safe
  • Stop further contact if safe
  • Tell them they are not in trouble
  • Take screenshots
  • Save usernames, links, and timestamps
  • Write down what happened
  • Do not delete anything before saving evidence
You do not need certainty before reporting — concern is enough.

When to use NCMEC CyberTipline

  • Online grooming or sexual communication
  • Requests for images or videos
  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
  • Sexual exploitation or trafficking concerns
  • Unwanted sexual content sent to a child

When to use IC3 (FBI)

  • Sextortion scams
  • Financial coercion or blackmail
  • Fraud linked to online contact
  • Broader cybercrime involving a child
IC3 supports cybercrime reporting, but child exploitation cases should still go to NCMEC.

When to involve police

  • There are threats or blackmail
  • Someone may meet the child in person
  • You know the person involved
  • The situation feels urgent or escalating
If risk is immediate or real-world, contact police alongside other reports.
