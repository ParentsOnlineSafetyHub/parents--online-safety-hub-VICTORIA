Report & Get Help — USA

If a child may be at risk in the United States, protect the child first, preserve evidence, and use the right reporting pathway quickly.

Immediate danger

If a child is in immediate danger or there is an urgent threat to safety, call 911 immediately.

If the danger is not immediate but the situation involves suspected online child sexual exploitation, preserve evidence and report it quickly.

Emergency first. Evidence second. Reporting next.

Main USA reporting pathways

NCMEC CyberTipline: for suspected online child sexual exploitation, enticement, CSAM, sex trafficking, and related abuse. 1

IC3: for cyber-enabled crime, fraud, sextortion-linked scams, and internet crime reporting to the FBI. 2

Local law enforcement / 911: for immediate safety threats, attempts to meet a child, or urgent physical risk. 3

What to do right away

  • Keep the child safe and stop further contact if safe to do so.
  • Tell the child they are not in trouble.
  • Save screenshots, usernames, profile links, and timestamps.
  • Write down what happened in simple notes.
  • Do not delete messages or accounts before preserving evidence.
NCMEC’s CyberTipline is the central U.S. reporting system for suspected online child sexual exploitation. 4

When to use NCMEC CyberTipline

  • Online enticement of a child for sexual acts
  • Child sexual abuse material
  • Child sexual molestation
  • Child sex trafficking
  • Unsolicited obscene material sent to a child
These are specifically listed in the CyberTipline reporting scope. 5

When to use IC3

IC3 is the FBI’s intake point for cyber-enabled crime complaints. It is useful when the incident also involves scams, extortion, fraud, or broader cybercrime concerns. 6
