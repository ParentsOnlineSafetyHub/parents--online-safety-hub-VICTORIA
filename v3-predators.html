Predators & Grooming

Calm awareness protects kids faster than panic.

The Golden Rule

No private chats with strangers.
If someone tries to move your child to another app, asks for secrecy, photos, voice, video, or “don’t tell your parents” — that’s a red flag.

How grooming usually works

  • Access: They find kids in games, group chats, live streams, comment sections.
  • Trust: Compliments, gifts, “you’re mature,” “I understand you,” “we’re best friends.”
  • Isolation: “Don’t tell your parents,” “they won’t understand,” “keep this secret.”
  • Escalation: Sexual talk, requests for photos, “dare games,” pressure, threats.
  • Migration: They lure kids off-platform to encrypted/private apps.

Red flags to watch for

  • Sudden secrecy: hiding screens, deleting chats, multiple accounts.
  • New “online friend” they refuse to explain.
  • Guilt/shame or fear when asked simple questions.
  • Odd sexual language for age, porn exposure, “jokes” about sex.
  • Requests to keep secrets, send pics, go on voice/video, or meet up.

Parent script (use this)

“You are not in trouble. You won’t lose your device for telling me the truth. If someone does anything weird online, we will handle it together — and we will report it.”
