Predators & Grooming

Predators follow patterns.
If you understand the pattern, you can stop it earlier.

This is how it starts
GROOMING DOES NOT LOOK DANGEROUS AT THE START
Most predators do not begin with obvious harm. They begin with friendliness, trust, and small steps that feel harmless at the time.
By the time it feels serious, the pattern is already underway.

The Golden Rule

If someone asks your child to move a conversation to Discord, Snapchat, Telegram, WhatsApp or Instagram DMs, treat it as a major red flag.

Children should tell a parent immediately.

Moving chats off-platform is one of the biggest escalation points
Child Safety First:
Grooming rarely stays in one place. It moves across games, chats, voice calls, private apps, and hidden communities.

How grooming usually happens

Most predators build trust first — not fear.

  • 1 — Access: finding children in games, chats, comments, or public spaces
  • 2 — Trust building: being friendly, helpful, and relatable
  • 3 — Isolation: moving to private messages or voice chat
  • 4 — Secrecy: encouraging the child not to tell anyone
  • 5 — Off-platform move: suggesting Discord, Snapchat, Telegram or similar
  • 6 — Control testing: checking if the child will follow instructions
  • 7 — Sexualisation: introducing sexual content or inappropriate topics
  • 8 — Exploitation: pressure, threats, sextortion, or manipulation
Grooming is usually slow and intentional — not random.

How the pattern escalates

Risk increases each time the contact becomes more private.

If contact becomes more private, more emotional, or more secretive — risk is increasing.

Common manipulation phrases

  • “Don’t tell your parents”
  • “You’re mature for your age”
  • “They wouldn’t understand”
  • “Add me on Discord / Snap”
  • “You can trust me”
  • “You’ll get in trouble if you tell”
These phrases are used to build secrecy, control, and emotional dependence.
