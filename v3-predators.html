Predators & Grooming

Know the tactics. Predators follow patterns — and patterns can be stopped.

The Golden Rule

If someone asks your child to move a conversation off the platform to Discord, Snapchat, Telegram, WhatsApp or Instagram DMs, that is a major grooming red flag.

Children should tell a parent immediately.

Moving chats off-platform is one of the biggest escalation points.

How grooming usually happens

Most predators do not start with obvious inappropriate behaviour. They usually build trust first.

  • 1 — Finding access: they search public games, servers, chats and comments where children interact.
  • 2 — Building trust: they act friendly, supportive, and mirror the child’s interests.
  • 3 — Isolation: they move the child into private messages or private voice/chat spaces.
  • 4 — Secrecy: they tell the child to keep conversations private.
  • 5 — Off-platform move: they suggest Discord, Snapchat, Telegram or another app.
  • 6 — Boundary testing: they test whether the child will follow instructions or stay quiet.
  • 7 — Sexualisation: they introduce sexual content, grooming language, shame, or “education.”
  • 8 — Exploitation: manipulation, pressure, sextortion, or blackmail may begin.
Important: grooming is rarely fast. It can take days, weeks, or months.

Common manipulation phrases

  • “Don’t tell your parents.”
  • “You’re mature for your age.”
  • “They won’t understand.”
  • “Add me on Discord / Snap.”
  • “You can trust me.”
  • “If you tell, you’ll get in trouble.”
