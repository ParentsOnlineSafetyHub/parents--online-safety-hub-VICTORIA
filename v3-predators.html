Predators & Grooming

Know the tactics. Predators follow patterns — and patterns can be stopped.

The Golden Rule

If someone asks your child to move a conversation off the platform to Discord, Snapchat, Telegram, WhatsApp or Instagram DMs, that is a major grooming red flag.

Children should tell a parent immediately.

Moving chats off-platform is one of the biggest escalation points
Child Safety First:
Grooming rarely stays in one place. It often moves across games, private chats, voice calls, disappearing-message apps, and hidden communities.

How grooming usually happens

Most predators do not start with obvious inappropriate behaviour. They usually build trust first.

  • 1 — Finding access: they search public games, servers, chats, comments, and platforms where children already interact.
  • 2 — Building trust: they act friendly, supportive, and mirror the child’s interests.
  • 3 — Isolation: they move the child into private messages or private voice and chat spaces.
  • 4 — Secrecy: they tell the child to keep conversations private.
  • 5 — Off-platform move: they suggest Discord, Snapchat, Telegram, WhatsApp, or another app.
  • 6 — Boundary testing: they test whether the child will follow instructions, stay quiet, or keep secrets.
  • 7 — Sexualisation: they introduce sexual content, grooming language, shame, or fake “education.”
  • 8 — Exploitation: manipulation, pressure, sextortion, threats, or blackmail may begin.
Important: grooming is rarely fast. It can take days, weeks, or months.

How the pattern usually escalates

The danger usually increases each time the contact becomes more private.

Public contact
↓
Private messaging
↓
Secrecy and emotional dependence
↓
Sexual pressure, threats, or exploitation
If the contact becomes more private, more secretive, or more emotionally intense, the risk is increasing.

Common manipulation phrases

  • “Don’t tell your parents.”
  • “You’re mature for your age.”
  • “They won’t understand.”
  • “Add me on Discord / Snap.”
  • “You can trust me.”
  • “If you tell, you’ll get in trouble.”
These phrases are not harmless. They are often used to create secrecy, control, and emotional dependence.
