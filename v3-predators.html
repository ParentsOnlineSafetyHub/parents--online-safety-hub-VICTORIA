Predators & Off-Platform Luring

They don’t start with danger. They start with trust.

The Off-Platform Rule

If a “friend” asks to move chats to another app — tell a parent immediately.

Discord • Snapchat • Telegram • WhatsApp • Instagram DMs — this is where predators isolate kids.

Why predators target gaming & social platforms

Predators go where children are. Big communities, voice chat, avatars, private messages, groups — these features make it easier to hide identity and build trust over time.

  • Avatars / usernames hide real identity.
  • Voice chat creates fast “friendship” and emotional connection.
  • Private chats reduce visibility and supervision.
  • Huge communities mean predators can blend in.

Common “move off platform” lines

Any version of these lines is a red flag — even if the person sounds the same age.

  • “Roblox chat sucks — add me on Discord.”
  • “Let’s talk on Snap, it’s easier.”
  • “My account might get banned, message me on Telegram.”
  • “Don’t tell your parents, they’ll ruin it.”
  • “Let’s jump into a private call.”

Platforms that need extra scrutiny

If it has DMs, groups, voice chat, live rooms, private worlds — it needs settings locked and supervision.

  • Roblox (chat + social contact + large child audience)
  • VRChat (voice chat + private worlds + mixed-age spaces)
  • Discord (servers + DMs + voice rooms)
  • Snapchat / Telegram / WhatsApp (private messaging)
  • YouTube / Shorts (algorithm exposure + comment risk depending on settings)

What to tell kids (simple and strong)

“You will never be in trouble for telling me. If someone online makes you uncomfortable, or asks you to keep secrets, we handle it together.”
