POSH

Age Guide

Age-appropriate boundaries reduce risk more than any single app setting.

Best order for every age

1) Device Safety → 2) Platforms → 3) Games / Social Media & Chat

Lock the system first, then the platform, then the app.

Children are safer when the rules are simple and repeated often

The POSH baseline (all ages)

1) Device controls ON (installs, screen time, filters).

2) No secret accounts. Parents can check anytime.

3) Location sharing OFF.

4) If it has chat / DMs / calls / groups — it must be locked down.

5) Devices charge overnight in a shared area.

Device Safety House Rules

Under 10

Goal: supervised learning + play only.

No public chat, no DMs, and no voice chat with strangers.

Friends should be real-life friends only and approved by a parent.

Use kid profiles or kid modes only.

A parent should be nearby for online games.

Parent script: “If someone you don’t know talks to you online, you tell me straight away.”

10–12

Goal: build habits + boundaries.

Private accounts by default.

DMs should be friends-only, or OFF where possible.

No group chats with unknown people.

Do a calm 5-minute settings check together each week.

Parent script: “If anyone asks you to move to Discord, Snapchat, or another app, that’s a red flag. You show me.”

13–15

Goal: guided independence.

Teach red flags: flattery, secrecy, gifts, moving to private chat, and “don’t tell your parents”.

Location sharing should stay OFF.

Review friend lists weekly in a calm, non-accusing way.

Platforms should be locked first, then games and apps.

Red Flags Predator Playbook Parent Scripts

16+

Goal: trust + accountability.

Keep the non-negotiables: no secret accounts, no private meets, and no sharing live location.

Teach: screenshot evidence, block and report fast, and ask for help early.

Freedom increases with honesty and consistency.

Parent script: “I’m not here to control you. I’m here to protect you from manipulation and exploitation.”

What POSH is really about

POSH is about child safety in the digital world.

That includes games, social media, private chats, livestreams, algorithm-driven content, misinformation, online grooming, manipulation, and digital communities.

The goal is simple: help parents reduce exposure, spot warning signs early, and make safer decisions for their children.
