Why age limits matter

Most harm happens in the gaps: open chat, open DMs, open profiles, and open access. This page gives a practical baseline for what’s appropriate by age — then you match your settings to it.

Non-negotiables (all ages)

1) If it has chat/DMs/voice calls — it gets locked down first.

2) No private contact with strangers.

3) Location sharing OFF.

4) Devices charge overnight in a shared area.

5) If someone says “keep this secret” → tell a parent immediately.

0–6 (Foundation)

Best fit: offline games, educational apps, parent-selected video only.
Avoid: social apps, online chat, public multiplayer.

Rules: shared spaces only • short sessions • no headphones • parent chooses content.

7–9 (Curiosity)

Best fit: supervised gaming with real-life friends only.
Avoid: open voice chat, public servers, random friend adds.

Rules: friends list = real friends • voice chat OFF • weekly setting check.

10–12 (Early independence)

This is a high-risk range for manipulation because kids want independence but don’t yet spot pressure tactics.

Rules: private accounts • DMs friends-only • no disappearing messages • devices charge in shared area.

13–15 (High exposure)

Teens will test boundaries. Don’t rely on trust alone — rely on structure + supervision.

Rules: private account • DMs friends-only • no meeting online contacts • weekly 5-minute check.

16–17 (Training adulthood)

Shift from control to judgement: teach reporting, boundaries, and early honesty.

Rules: no sharing intimate content • no meeting online contacts alone • tell a parent early if something feels off.

Match your settings to your rules

If the rule is “no DMs”, but DMs are ON, the rule will fail. Lock devices first, then platforms, then apps/games.
