Why Discord Is High Exposure

Discord is not just a chat app. It is:

• Private servers • Public invite links • Voice chat rooms • Direct messages • Anonymous usernames • File sharing • Cross-community access

Private server + anonymous access = increased contact risk.

The Real Issue

Parents often assume Discord is “just for gaming”. In reality, it functions like a private social network.

Without strict settings, strangers can: • DM your child • Invite them to private servers • Share inappropriate material

Step-By-Step: Reduce Discord Risk

1) Disable Direct Messages From Server Members
User Settings → Privacy & Safety → Turn OFF “Allow DMs from server members”

2) Enable Safe Direct Messaging
Privacy & Safety → Safe Direct Messaging → Keep Me Safe

3) Restrict Friend Requests
Settings → Friend Requests → Disable “Everyone”

4) Review Joined Servers
Remove any unknown or adult-heavy communities.

5) Turn Off Message Requests
Limit who can message directly.

6) Monitor Weekly
Discord changes quickly. Regular review is essential.

Proactive Protection

Silence and ignorance increase risk. Calm supervision reduces it.
