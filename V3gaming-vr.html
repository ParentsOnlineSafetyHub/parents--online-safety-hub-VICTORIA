Why parents miss VR risk

VR looks like just another game system.

But it behaves more like a live social environment where strangers can speak, approach, and interact directly.

The risk is not just the game — it’s the live interaction inside it

Associated VR platforms & worlds

VR worlds allow players to interact through voice, movement, avatars, and shared environments. Some spaces are not designed for children at all.

What parents should lock down first

  • Voice chat and microphone access
  • Who can approach or interact in worlds
  • Public vs private room access
  • Friend requests and social connections
  • Time spent in open/public environments
  • Movement between worlds and communities
In VR, strangers can feel like they are “right next to” your child — even when they are not.

How VR risk can escalate

Public world interaction
↓
Voice conversation starts
↓
Repeated interaction or bonding
↓
Move to private instance or group
↓
Secrecy, pressure, or off-platform contact
VR speeds up connection. That also means it can speed up manipulation.

Extra controls parents should use

  • Disable or limit microphone use where possible
  • Restrict access to public worlds
  • Keep play in supervised or known environments
  • Review friends and recent interactions regularly
  • Avoid unsupervised “world hopping”
  • Check which communities your child is joining
Children often forget they are speaking to strangers because VR feels real.

Important warning about VRChat

VRChat is one of the most open VR platforms available.

Public worlds allow anyone to join, including adults interacting with children in real time.

  • Unmoderated public voice chat is common
  • Adult-themed worlds and avatars exist
  • Private instances can be created easily
  • External Discord servers are often used
  • Avatars can act out inappropriate behaviour
VRChat is generally not recommended for unsupervised children

Early warning signs in VR use

  • Child becomes highly attached to one VR “friend”
  • More secrecy about who they are interacting with
  • Defensiveness when asked about VR activity
  • Late-night or extended sessions in private worlds
  • Emotional changes after VR use
