The Guide

Prevention is better than panic. Simple boundaries, safer settings, calmer action.

Why this guide matters

Children now grow up in a world of games, social apps, livestreams, private chats, algorithms, and constant digital access.

The goal is not fear. The goal is reducing exposure, building trust, and helping parents act early with clear steps.

The biggest risks usually grow through access, privacy, and repeated contact
Child Safety First:
This guide is built to help parents reduce risk quickly without needing to become tech experts first.

Use this guide as your starting pathway

If you feel overwhelmed, do not try to learn every app, game, and platform today. Start with the basics that reduce exposure fastest, then move deeper only where needed.

1. The 10-minute setup

  • Set all accounts to Private
  • Set messaging to Friends Only where possible
  • Disable voice chat for younger children
  • Turn off location sharing
  • Have devices charge overnight in a shared family area
Device Controls Platforms Safety Check

2. Family online rules

  • No moving chats to new apps without parent approval
  • No sharing personal details like school, suburb, routines, or address
  • No sending photos to online-only contacts
  • Weekly calm check-in: “Anything weird happen online?”
  • Children need to know they will not be punished for telling the truth
House Rules Parent Scripts Parent Mindset

3. Warning signs of grooming or manipulation

  • Requests for secrecy
  • Gift offers such as skins, credits, money, Robux, or items
  • Encouraging the child to move the chat to another platform
  • Requests for photos, videos, or voice calls
  • Flattery that becomes personal, sexual, or emotionally controlling
One of the clearest danger signs is when someone tries to move a child from a visible space into a more private one.

4. If you are concerned

  • Stay calm
  • Screenshot everything: usernames, dates, messages, links, and profiles
  • Preserve evidence before blocking if possible
  • Block and report within the platform
  • If there is immediate danger, contact emergency services straight away
Calm action protects better than panic. A child who feels safe is more likely to keep telling the truth.

5. Ongoing prevention

  • Review privacy settings regularly
  • Keep devices and apps updated
  • Maintain open communication, not just one big talk
  • Model healthy digital behaviour as adults too
  • Keep checking the apps, games, and platforms your child actually uses
