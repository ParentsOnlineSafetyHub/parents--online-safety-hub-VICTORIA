POSH Response System

When something feels off, parents need a process. Not panic. Not guesswork. A process.

The system

POSH is built around one simple response pattern:

Notice
↓
Check
↓
Secure
↓
Talk
↓
Monitor
↓
Escalate if needed
Structure beats panic

1) Notice

Start with what changed.

  • Secrecy
  • Deleted chats
  • Emotional shifts
  • Late-night contact
  • Hidden accounts
  • One person suddenly matters too much
You do not need full proof to begin paying attention.

2) Check

Check calmly, not aggressively.

  • Devices
  • Apps
  • Privacy settings
  • Friends lists
  • Recent downloads
  • Server invites and usernames
Check a Device Calmly Device Safety

3) Secure

Reduce access before the problem deepens.

  • Lock down risky communication settings
  • Pause unsafe apps or games
  • Use device controls
  • Prevent further contact where necessary
  • Preserve evidence before anything disappears

4) Talk

Do not lead with attack. Lead with safety.

  • Stay calm
  • State what you noticed
  • Make it clear they are not in trouble for telling the truth
  • Focus on safety before blame
Parent Scripts If Your Child Opens Up

5) Monitor

Do not assume one conversation fixes it.

  • Watch patterns over time
  • Review friends and apps regularly
  • Look for returning secrecy
  • Keep the door open for more disclosure

6) Escalate if needed

If there is sexual contact, blackmail, coercion, threats, explicit material, or real-world meeting risk, escalate immediately.

Bottom line

Parents do not need to know everything in the first moment.

They need a structure that moves them from confusion into action.

Notice it. Check it. Secure it. Talk. Monitor. Escalate if needed.
