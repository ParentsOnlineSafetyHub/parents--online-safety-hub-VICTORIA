Real Life Risk & Grooming Signs

Focus on behaviour, not assumptions. Patterns matter more than appearances.

Important:
There is no reliable way to “identify” someone by appearance alone.
What matters are patterns of behaviour, access, and manipulation.

Many harmful individuals do not look suspicious. They often appear friendly, helpful, trusted, or respected.

Why this is hard to recognise

Most people expect danger to look obvious. In reality, it often looks normal.

Concern should come from patterns of behaviour, not how someone presents.

Behaviour patterns to watch for

  • Trying to build a special or secret relationship with a child
  • Giving gifts, money, or favours without clear reason
  • Seeking one-on-one time away from others
  • Encouraging secrecy from parents or caregivers
  • Overly interested in a child’s personal life or emotions
  • Contacting a child frequently (messages, calls, online)
  • Trying to become the “trusted adult” quickly
  • Gradually crossing boundaries (jokes, topics, behaviour)
Grooming is usually a process over time, not a single obvious moment.

Signs a child may be affected

  • Sudden secrecy around devices or messages
  • Emotional changes (withdrawn, anxious, defensive)
  • New “friend” they won’t explain clearly
  • Reluctance to talk about certain people
  • Receiving unexplained gifts or money
  • Trying to hide conversations or apps
Changes in behaviour are often the first visible sign something is wrong.

If something feels off

You do not need proof to take early protective steps.

What To Do Now Online Red Flags Report in Australia
Trust your instincts — but act calmly and gather information.

How to respond safely

  • Stay calm and avoid panic reactions
  • Do not confront the person directly
  • Talk to the child in a supportive, non-judgmental way
  • Gather information and preserve evidence
  • Limit further contact where appropriate
  • Seek professional or legal guidance if needed

Important legal reminder

Do not publicly accuse or name individuals without verified legal findings.

False accusations can cause serious harm and legal consequences.
