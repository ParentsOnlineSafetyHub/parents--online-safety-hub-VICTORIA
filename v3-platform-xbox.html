Xbox

Xbox connects players through messaging, parties, and multiplayer games. Lock communication settings first.

Why Xbox settings matter

Xbox allows messaging, voice chat, party chat, and multiplayer contact across many games.

Without parental controls, strangers can contact your child more easily than many parents realise.

Messaging + party chat + multiplayer = exposure

Important Xbox safety settings

1) Restrict messages from strangers

2) Control who can communicate with your child

3) Disable friend requests from unknown players where possible

4) Lock spending through Microsoft Family Safety

5) Review voice and party chat permissions

Console settings should be locked before assuming a game is safe enough on its own.

Common Xbox games parents should check

Fortnite Minecraft Roblox Call of Duty GTA Online Rocket League Apex Legends Rainbow Six Siege

What parents should watch for

  • Strangers sending friend requests after matches
  • Children joining party chat with unknown players
  • Repeated contact from the same player
  • Players asking to move to Discord or Snapchat
  • Late-night online sessions with regular unknown players

Why Xbox can become risky

  • Party chat can make strangers feel familiar very quickly.
  • One match can turn into repeated team play and private group contact.
  • Children may build loyalty to teammates they have never met in real life.
  • Communication often moves from Xbox into Discord, Snapchat, or other private apps.
The biggest risk is usually not the console itself. It is the communication pathways built around games, parties, and friend systems.

Quick reminder for parents

Xbox can feel safer because it is a console at home.

But once messaging, party chat, and online multiplayer begin, the risks can look very similar to other platforms.

Check the communication settings, not just the device type
