The core idea

POSH is exploring a child-safety accountability agreement for adults who want to be recognised as part of a safer family, community, club, school, or support network.

The purpose is not to accuse people. The purpose is to create clear written boundaries around children, private contact, secrecy, grooming behaviour, digital communication, and adult responsibility.

A safe adult should not be afraid of clear child-safety boundaries.

What this agreement is meant to do

Create clarity

It sets clear expectations about secrecy, private contact, digital communication, gifts, pressure, emotional manipulation, and child-safe behaviour.

Support prevention

It helps families and organisations talk about risk before there is a crisis, accusation, disclosure, or harm.

Record responsibility

It creates a signed record that the adult understood and accepted child-safety boundaries before being trusted around children.

What this agreement must not become

This concept must stay responsible, lawful, fair, and child-focused.

Not a public accusation system

Refusing to sign does not prove guilt, risk, or wrongdoing. It only means the person is not verified under this optional POSH accountability process.

Not a replacement for official checks

This does not replace police checks, Working With Children Checks, mandatory reporting, child protection processes, court orders, or safeguarding policies.

Not a sentencing contract

A private agreement cannot force courts to double a sentence. The stronger approach is to record prior knowledge, accepted responsibility, and breach of trust.

Not a guarantee of safety

A signed agreement does not prove someone is safe. It is one layer of visibility, culture, boundaries, and accountability.

Draft Safe Adult Accountability Agreement

This wording is draft-only and should be reviewed by legal, child-safety, safeguarding, and community stakeholders before use.

1. Identity declaration

I confirm that the identity information I provide for this agreement is true and accurate to the best of my knowledge.

2. Child-safety status declaration

I declare that I am not knowingly prohibited, disqualified, suspended, restricted, or legally barred from working with, supervising, transporting, coaching, mentoring, babysitting, contacting, or being alone with children.

3. No secrecy with children

I will not ask, encourage, pressure, reward, threaten, manipulate, or instruct a child to keep secrets from their parent, carer, guardian, teacher, police, child protection, or another safe adult.

4. No hidden private contact

I will not privately message, contact, gift, pressure, groom, sexualise, threaten, blackmail, isolate, or move a child to another platform or private channel without parent-approved visibility and appropriate safeguards.

5. No grooming behaviour

I understand that gifts, special attention, emotional dependency, secrecy, isolation, deleted messages, sexual comments, shame, threats, blackmail, hidden accounts, off-platform movement, or encouraging distrust of safe adults may be treated as warning signs.

6. Digital conduct responsibility

I will not use games, apps, livestreams, private messages, group chats, disappearing messages, voice chat, social media, or online communities to create unsafe access to a child.

7. Reporting responsibility

If I become aware that a child may be unsafe, groomed, exploited, blackmailed, sexually pressured, threatened, harmed, or placed at risk, I will raise the concern with the appropriate parent, carer, guardian, safeguarding lead, child protection service, police, or relevant authority.

8. Accountability acknowledgement

I understand that if I breach this agreement, the agreement may be provided to relevant parents, carers, guardians, organisations, investigators, police, child protection, courts, regulators, or safeguarding bodies where lawful and appropriate.

9. Consequence acknowledgement

I accept that a breach of this agreement may result in immediate removal from a trusted role, restriction of access to children, parent or organisation notification, evidence preservation, and referral to appropriate authorities.

10. Strongest lawful consequences statement

I support the strongest lawful consequences available for any adult who abuses, grooms, exploits, coerces, blackmails, sexualises, threatens, harms, or endangers a child, especially where that adult used trust, authority, secrecy, or access to create harm.

Plain-language definitions

Safe adult

A person who acts transparently, respects boundaries, does not create secrecy with children, and supports child safety even when it is inconvenient.

Trusted access

Any situation where an adult is allowed around a child through family trust, community trust, coaching, mentoring, babysitting, online contact, transport, activities, or supervision.

Grooming behaviour

A pattern of building access, trust, secrecy, emotional dependency, isolation, pressure, or fear so a child becomes easier to manipulate or harm.

Hidden private contact

Contact with a child that is concealed from parents, carers, guardians, safe adults, or proper supervision, including private messages, secret accounts, deleted chats, or off-platform movement.

Off-platform movement

Moving a child from a visible or supervised space into another app, game, chat, direct message, private group, encrypted platform, or harder-to-monitor space.

Breach of trust

When an adult uses a trusted role, relationship, position, access, authority, or emotional influence in a way that places a child at risk.
