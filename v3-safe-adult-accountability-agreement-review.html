The core idea

POSH is exploring a child-safety accountability agreement for adults who want to voluntarily support safer family, community, club, school, online, or support-network environments.

The purpose is not to accuse people. The purpose is to create clear written expectations around children, private contact, secrecy, grooming behaviour, digital communication, gifts, pressure, authority, trust, and adult responsibility.

The goal is a safer culture where adults understand the boundaries clearly, children are easier to protect, and concerns can be handled earlier, calmly, lawfully, and responsibly.

What this agreement is meant to do

Create clarity

It sets clear expectations around secrecy, private contact, digital communication, gifts, pressure, emotional manipulation, and child-safe behaviour.

Support prevention

It helps families, clubs, groups, and organisations talk about risk before there is a crisis, accusation, disclosure, or harm.

Record responsibility

It creates a written record that a person was shown the child-safety expectations and chose to acknowledge those boundaries.

What this agreement must not become

This concept must stay responsible, lawful, fair, privacy-aware, and child-focused.

Not a public accusation system

Refusing to sign does not prove guilt, risk, dishonesty, or wrongdoing. People may refuse for many lawful reasons, including privacy, employment, legal advice, religious advice, misunderstanding, or concern about misuse.

Not a replacement for official checks

This does not replace police checks, Working With Children Checks, mandatory reporting, child protection processes, court orders, safeguarding policies, professional supervision, or official reporting pathways.

Not a sentencing contract

A private agreement cannot force a court to double a sentence. The safer wording is that the adult acknowledges child-safety expectations and supports the strongest lawful consequences for harm against children.

Not a guarantee of safety

A signed agreement does not prove someone is safe. It is only one possible layer of culture, visibility, boundaries, accountability, and prevention.

Not a data collection system yet

No identity documents, sensitive information, signatures, declarations, or background details should be collected until privacy, consent, storage, access, deletion, and legal review processes are properly designed.

Not a vigilante tool

This concept must not encourage harassment, doxxing, public shaming, threats, unlawful exposure, retaliation, or private punishment. Concerns about child safety should be handled through lawful safeguarding and reporting pathways.

Draft Safe Adult Accountability Agreement

This wording is draft-only and should be reviewed by legal, safeguarding, privacy, child-safety, community, and organisational stakeholders before use.

1. Identity declaration

I confirm that any identity information I provide for this agreement is true and accurate to the best of my knowledge.

2. Child-safety status declaration

I declare that, to the best of my knowledge, I am not prohibited, disqualified, suspended, restricted, or legally barred from working with, supervising, transporting, coaching, mentoring, babysitting, contacting, or being alone with children.

3. No secrecy with children

I will not ask, encourage, pressure, reward, threaten, manipulate, or instruct a child to keep secrets from their parent, carer, guardian, teacher, safeguarding lead, police, child protection service, or another safe adult.

4. No hidden private contact

I will not privately message, contact, gift, pressure, groom, sexualise, threaten, blackmail, isolate, or move a child to another platform or private channel without appropriate parent-approved visibility, role-based approval, and child-safety safeguards.

5. No grooming behaviour

I understand that gifts, special attention, emotional dependency, secrecy, isolation, deleted messages, sexual comments, shame, threats, blackmail, hidden accounts, off-platform movement, or encouraging distrust of safe adults may be treated as warning signs requiring concern, review, or action.

6. Digital conduct responsibility

I will not use games, apps, livestreams, private messages, group chats, disappearing messages, voice chat, social media, online communities, or digital platforms to create unsafe access to a child.

7. Gifts, favours, and special treatment

I will not use gifts, money, digital currency, game items, rewards, favours, attention, secrets, emotional dependence, or special treatment to influence, isolate, pressure, or control a child.

8. Physical and real-world boundaries

I will not create unnecessary one-on-one isolation, secret meetings, hidden transport arrangements, unsupervised access, or private situations with a child outside appropriate parent, carer, guardian, organisational, or safeguarding approval.

9. Reporting responsibility

If I become aware that a child may be unsafe, groomed, exploited, blackmailed, sexually pressured, threatened, harmed, isolated, manipulated, or placed at risk, I will raise the concern with the appropriate parent, carer, guardian, safeguarding lead, child protection service, police, or relevant authority.

10. Evidence responsibility

If a serious concern arises, I will not destroy, hide, alter, forward, publish, mock, or misuse evidence. I will preserve relevant information where safe and lawful, and I will follow appropriate reporting guidance.

11. Accountability acknowledgement

I understand that if I breach this agreement, the agreement may be provided to relevant parents, carers, guardians, organisations, investigators, police, child protection services, courts, regulators, or safeguarding bodies where lawful and appropriate.

12. Consequence acknowledgement

I accept that a breach of this agreement may result in removal from a trusted role, restriction of access to children, parent or organisation notification, evidence preservation, safeguarding review, and referral to appropriate authorities where lawful and appropriate.

13. Strongest lawful consequences statement

I support the strongest lawful consequences available for any adult who abuses, grooms, exploits, coerces, blackmails, sexualises, threatens, harms, or endangers a child, especially where that adult used trust, authority, secrecy, access, or influence to create harm.

Plain-language definitions

Safe adult

A person who acts transparently, respects boundaries, does not create secrecy with children, supports child safety, and accepts appropriate accountability when trusted around children.

Trusted access

Any situation where an adult is allowed around a child through family trust, community trust, coaching, mentoring, babysitting, online contact, transport, activities, supervision, authority, or support.

Grooming behaviour

A pattern of building access, trust, secrecy, emotional dependency, isolation, pressure, or fear so a child becomes easier to manipulate, exploit, control, or harm.

Hidden private contact

Contact with a child that is concealed from parents, carers, guardians, safe adults, or proper supervision, including private messages, secret accounts, deleted chats, or off-platform movement.

Off-platform movement

Moving a child from a visible or supervised space into another app, game, direct message, private group, encrypted platform, disappearing-message platform, or harder-to-monitor space.

Breach of trust

When an adult uses a trusted role, relationship, position, access, authority, or emotional influence in a way that places a child at risk.

Feedback requested

Legal review

Is the wording fair, lawful, careful, and unlikely to create defamation, discrimination, privacy, employment, or misuse risks?

Child-safety review

Privacy review

Parent review

Organisation review

Misuse review

How could this be misused if written poorly, and what safeguards should be built in before any public release?
