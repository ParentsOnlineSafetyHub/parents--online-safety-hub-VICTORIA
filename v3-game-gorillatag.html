Gorilla Tag

One of the most popular VR games for kids — and almost entirely voice-based interaction.

Why Gorilla Tag matters

Gorilla Tag uses open voice chat where children often interact freely with strangers.

Because the game looks simple and cartoon-like, many parents assume it is low risk.

VR voice chat means real-time conversations with strangers

Why VR games can be different

Virtual Reality games like Gorilla Tag feel more immersive than normal games.

Players move their bodies, speak naturally, and interact in ways that feel closer to real-life conversations.

That immersion can make children more comfortable talking to strangers than they would in a normal game chat.

When children feel like they are “in the same space” as other players, trust can build much faster.

Common risks in Gorilla Tag

  • Open voice chat with unknown players
  • Players asking children to join private rooms
  • Older players interacting heavily with younger children
  • Discord servers linked to VR communities
  • Children forming regular voice friendships with strangers

Safety precautions

1) Monitor play sessions when possible

2) Disable voice chat where the platform allows it

3) Limit friend requests

4) Check who your child regularly plays with

5) Set a rule: no moving conversations to Discord or other apps

Red flags in Gorilla Tag

  • Older players befriending children repeatedly
  • Players asking children to join private rooms
  • Requests to move conversations to Discord
  • Players asking personal questions about age, location, or school
  • Encouraging secrecy from parents
