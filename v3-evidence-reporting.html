Evidence & Reporting

Save the right things. Report the right way. Avoid mistakes that can remove evidence or weaken your position.

Why this matters

In many cases, evidence disappears quickly. Accounts get deleted, messages vanish, and platforms remove content.

If the right details are not saved early, it can make reporting harder later.

What you save early can matter more than what you remember later

What to save (keep it simple)

You are not building a perfect case file. You are capturing key details before they disappear.

  • Screenshots of chats, messages, or conversations
  • Usernames, display names, and profile pages
  • App or platform name (Roblox, Discord, Snapchat, etc.)
  • Dates and times (if visible)
  • Links, group names, or server names
  • Any threats, requests, or suspicious behaviour
  • Images or files sent (do not alter them)
Tip: Take multiple screenshots rather than trying to capture everything perfectly in one.

What NOT to do when saving evidence

  • Do not delete chats or accounts too early
  • Do not edit screenshots or crop out details
  • Do not confront the person first if reporting may be needed
  • Do not rely on memory instead of saving proof
Once something is deleted, it may not be recoverable.

Where to report (Australia)

Reporting depends on what is happening. Use the right channel for the situation.

🚨 Immediate danger or real-world risk:
Contact police immediately (000)

🛑 Online child exploitation or grooming:
Report to the eSafety Commissioner

⚠️ Platform-based issues:
Report directly inside the app (Roblox, Discord, Snapchat, etc.)

📄 Ongoing or serious concerns:
Consider police reporting alongside platform reporting

You can report to more than one place if needed.

How to report (without making mistakes)

Reporting does not need to be perfect. It needs to be clear, factual, and supported by what you saved.

  • Stick to facts — what happened, where, and when
  • Include usernames and platform names
  • Attach screenshots or evidence where possible
  • Avoid assumptions or emotional language
  • Keep a copy of what you submit
Clear, simple reporting is stronger than long emotional explanations.

What parents often get wrong

  • Deleting everything too early
  • Confronting the person before saving evidence
  • Waiting too long hoping it stops
  • Not reporting because they are unsure
If something feels wrong, it is better to report early than regret waiting.
