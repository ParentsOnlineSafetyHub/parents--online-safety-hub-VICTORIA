Evidence & Reporting

Save the right things. Report the right way. This page is built for parents worldwide, with Australian options included clearly below.

Why this matters

Evidence can disappear quickly. Messages get deleted, accounts vanish, and platforms remove content.

If the right details are not saved early, reporting can become harder later.

What you save early can matter more than what you remember later
If the risk feels active right now:
Protect the child first, save what matters, and report in the right order. You do not need every answer before you begin.

What to save (keep it simple)

You are not building a perfect case file. You are capturing key details before they disappear.

  • Screenshots of chats, messages, comments, or conversations
  • Usernames, display names, and profile pages
  • App or platform name (Roblox, Discord, Snapchat, TikTok, etc.)
  • Dates and times, if visible
  • Links, group names, server names, or channel names
  • Threats, requests, secrecy, blackmail, or suspicious behaviour
  • Images or files sent, without altering them
Tip: Take multiple screenshots rather than trying to capture everything perfectly in one.

What NOT to do when saving evidence

  • Do not delete chats or accounts too early
  • Do not edit screenshots or crop out important details
  • Do not confront the person first if reporting may be needed
  • Do not rely on memory instead of saving proof
  • Do not wipe the device before preserving what matters
Once something is deleted, it may not be recoverable.

Where to report worldwide

The exact reporting body depends on your country, but the general structure stays the same.

🚨 Immediate danger or real-world risk:
Contact your local emergency police service immediately.

⚠️ Platform-based abuse, threats, impersonation, grooming contact, or harmful content:
Report it inside the app or service first.

🛑 Suspected child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or serious exploitation content:
Use your national hotline if one exists. INHOPE provides a global hotline network and reporting portal for suspected CSAM.

📄 If your country has a dedicated national exploitation reporting body:
Report there as well, especially for grooming, coercion, sexual extortion, or attempts to meet a child offline.

In many cases, the safest approach is: platform report + national hotline/authority + police if there is immediate risk.

Australia

Australia has official reporting pathways depending on the type of harm.

🚨 Immediate danger:
Call 000.

🛑 Online child sexual exploitation, grooming, inappropriate contact, sextortion, or attempts to meet a child:
Report to ACCCE.

⚠️ Serious online abuse, image-based abuse, cyberbullying of a child, or illegal and restricted online content:
Use eSafety if the matter fits what eSafety investigates. Some eSafety complaints require reporting to the platform first.

🚓 Non-emergency police help:
Contact your local police or Police Assistance on 131 444 where appropriate.

United States

In the United States, NCMEC’s CyberTipline is the central reporting system for online child sexual exploitation.

🚨 Immediate danger:
Call 911.

🛑 Online enticement, sextortion, CSAM, exploitation, or related concerns involving a child:
Report to the CyberTipline.

⚠️ Platform-based issues:
Report inside the app or service as well.

How to report (without making mistakes)

Reporting does not need to be perfect. It needs to be clear, factual, and supported by what you saved.

  • Stick to facts: what happened, where it happened, and when
  • Include usernames, profile names, platform names, and links where possible
  • Attach screenshots or saved evidence when appropriate
  • Avoid assumptions, guesses, or long emotional explanations
  • Keep a copy of what you submit
  • Save any reference number or confirmation email
Clear, simple reporting is usually stronger than long emotional reporting.

What parents often get wrong

  • Deleting everything too early
  • Confronting the person before saving evidence
  • Waiting too long and hoping it stops
  • Reporting with too little detail because nothing was saved
  • Not reporting because they are unsure whether it is serious enough
If something feels wrong, it is usually better to preserve first and report early than regret waiting.

If the child is involved in the report

Keep the child calm and avoid making them feel blamed. The child needs safety, reassurance, and a sense that telling the truth helps.

Say: “You’re not in trouble. I’m glad you told me. We’re going to handle this properly.”
