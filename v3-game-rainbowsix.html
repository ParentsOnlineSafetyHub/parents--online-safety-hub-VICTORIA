Rainbow Six Siege

Highly tactical team play often pushes players toward voice chat and outside communities.

Why parents should know Rainbow Six Siege

Siege relies heavily on communication and teamwork, which increases voice chat exposure and invites to private groups.

Children may be encouraged to join Discords, squads, or regular groups very quickly.

Team strategy often becomes private contact outside the game

Why Rainbow Six Siege can become risky

  • Team success often depends on repeated communication with the same players.
  • Voice chat can make trust build quickly with strangers.
  • Players are often encouraged to join regular squads, parties, or Discord servers.
  • Older players can become regular contacts through repeated team play.
  • Communication may shift from the game into private voice channels or off-platform communities.
The biggest risk is usually not the tactics or violence. It is the communication and repeated contact that grows around the game.

Important safety settings

1) Restrict voice chat if possible

2) Limit friend requests and squad invites

3) Use platform privacy settings first

4) Monitor regular teammate contact

5) Keep younger children out of open team voice channels where possible

Red flags in Rainbow Six Siege

  • Discord or private server invites
  • Older players seeking repeated contact with a child
  • Requests to keep team chats private from parents
  • Pressure to join off-platform voice channels
  • Repeated invites from the same player or squad
  • Requests for age, location, socials, or personal details

What parents should do

1) Ask whether your child is using voice chat with known friends or strangers

2) Check whether team contact is staying inside the game or moving elsewhere

3) Set a clear rule: no moving game contact into private apps without parent knowledge

4) Watch for regular players becoming off-platform contacts
