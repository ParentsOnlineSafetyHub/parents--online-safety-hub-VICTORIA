Fortnite

Fortnite is more than just a game. It is a social multiplayer space with voice chat, party systems, friend requests, and cross-platform play that can connect children to strangers quickly.

Why parents should know Fortnite

Fortnite is one of the most common games children use to talk, team up, and socialise online.

Because it feels fun and familiar, many children become comfortable chatting with people they do not actually know.

Fun gameplay can still create real access to strangers

Online connection is not always negative

I met my fiancée playing Fortnite through mutual friends. We spent time online together, and after a few weeks we realised we were no longer just playing the game — we were building a real connection.

Today, we have a beautiful daughter together, along with our four other children from previous relationships.

That is why POSH is not anti-connection.
It is pro-awareness, pro-boundaries, and pro-child safety.

Important Fortnite safety settings

1) Disable or restrict voice chat with strangers

2) Restrict friend requests where possible

3) Turn off open party join permissions

4) Enable Epic parental controls

5) Review platform privacy settings on console, PC, or mobile

6) Keep younger children in known friend groups where possible

Why Fortnite can become risky

  • Children can play with strangers through squad fills and public matchmaking.
  • Voice chat makes fast relationship-building easier.
  • Cross-platform play means the child may be talking to adults on PC, console, or mobile.
  • Players often move from Fortnite into Discord, Snapchat, or other private apps.
  • In-game gifting and skins can be used to build trust.
The biggest risk is usually not the graphics — it is the communication and movement into private spaces.

Red flags in Fortnite

  • Players inviting children into private voice chats
  • Requests to join Discord servers
  • Players asking for personal information
  • Players offering in-game gifts or rewards
  • Repeated invites from the same stranger
  • Pressure to keep conversations private
  • Children becoming emotionally attached to an online teammate they have never met

How contact often escalates

What starts as a normal match can gradually become private contact.

Play one match together
↓
Add as friend
↓
Voice chat becomes regular
↓
Private party chats
↓
Move to Discord or another app
If contact keeps moving further away from the game itself, the risk usually increases.

What parents should do

1) Ask whether your child plays with real-life friends or strangers

2) Check whether voice chat is enabled

3) Set a rule: no moving Fortnite contact into private apps without parent knowledge

4) Watch for gifting, repeated invites, or emotional reliance on online teammates
