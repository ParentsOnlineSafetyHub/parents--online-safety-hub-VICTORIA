POSH

Algorithm Awareness

Understand recommendation systems so you can reduce exposure and risk.

Why this matters

Modern apps don’t show content randomly. They use recommendation systems designed to maximise attention and engagement. Kids are more vulnerable because they’re still building impulse control, critical thinking, and digital awareness.

How recommendation systems work

They commonly use signals like:

  • Watch time and replays
  • Clicks and how quickly a user engages
  • What similar viewers watched next
  • Comment activity and interactions
  • Session length (keeping you watching longer)

These systems don’t verify maturity — they respond to behaviour patterns.

Exposure creep

A child can begin with age-appropriate content and gradually be recommended:

  • Older creators and older audiences
  • Teen-targeted humour and themes
  • Comment-heavy videos and livestreams
  • Content that encourages off-platform movement

This shift is subtle and happens gradually — most parents never notice it in real time.

Short-form video risks

Short-form vertical video formats (Shorts, Reels, Spotlight, Clips) can increase:

  • Endless scrolling behaviour
  • Fast dopamine reinforcement cycles
  • Reduced thinking time between content
  • Rapid exposure to older audiences

The format — not just the platform — creates risk.

Comment and livestream vulnerabilities

  • Public usernames visible to strangers
  • Reply chains that create familiarity
  • Live chat exposure and rapid interaction
  • Migration to private platforms (Discord/Telegram)

Predatory behaviour often exploits open comment environments and cross-platform movement.

How parents disrupt the cycle

  • Disable autoplay
  • Review watch history weekly
  • Use supervised / child accounts
  • Limit Shorts/Reels for younger kids
  • Reset watch history when needed
  • Have calm, regular conversations about online experiences

The POSH principle

Algorithms optimise for engagement. Parents optimise for safety.
