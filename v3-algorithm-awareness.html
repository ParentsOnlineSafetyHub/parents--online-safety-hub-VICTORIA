Algorithm Risks

What kids click changes what they see. Algorithms can push harmful content fast.

Why algorithms matter

Algorithms reward attention, not safety.

The more a child watches, clicks, pauses on, or reacts to content, the more similar content gets pushed back at them.

A few clicks can quickly change a child’s feed

What parents need to know

  • Algorithms can push sexual, violent, or manipulative content
  • Children may be fed more extreme versions of what they already clicked
  • Predators and harmful creators exploit trends, hashtags, and youth interests
  • Even passive watching can teach the platform what to show next

Signs the feed is becoming unsafe

  • Sudden sexualised content appearing often
  • Gore, shock videos, or violent clips
  • Harmful “jokes” normalised over time
  • Strange communities, hashtags, or creator suggestions

What to do

1) Reset or clean up the feed by using “not interested” tools

2) Review watch history and followed accounts

3) Use private profiles, filters, and safer search settings

4) Talk with your child about how feeds learn from behaviour

Why algorithms matter for parents

  • Algorithms push content designed to keep people engaged longer.
  • Children are often recommended more extreme or sensational content over time.
  • Online communities can reinforce behaviours and beliefs quickly.
  • Gaming, social apps, and streaming platforms all use similar engagement systems.
  • Predators often take advantage of these environments where kids are already spending time.
