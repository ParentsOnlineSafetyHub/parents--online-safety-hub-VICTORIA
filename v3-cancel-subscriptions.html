Cancel Subscriptions

Reduce access, reduce risk. If an app or platform is no longer safe, cut it off fast.

Why this matters

If a child no longer needs access to an app, game, or paid platform, cancel it.

Less access means fewer pathways for strangers, fewer hidden purchases, and fewer repeat risks.

Removing access is sometimes the safest option

When to cancel or remove access

  • The app is repeatedly causing safety problems
  • The child is too young for the current exposure level
  • Unknown people are gaining private access
  • There are repeated spending issues or hidden purchases
  • Parent supervision is not realistic for that platform

Best order

1) Cancel the subscription

2) Remove the app or game

3) Change passwords if needed

4) Rebuild safer rules before restoring access
