Why Teams needs supervision

Teams is often used for school. Risks usually come from chat, file sharing, and contact outside approved groups.

Step-by-step: safer Teams use

1) Use school-managed accounts where possible
School accounts typically have admin controls enabled.

2) Check chat permissions
If your child can message outside school channels, reduce it (school admin may control this).

3) Meetings: who can present
In meeting options: set “Who can present” to Organizer only where possible.

4) Disable anonymous join (if available)
Use settings to prevent unknown users joining meetings.

5) Device controls
Set time limits and keep Teams use inside learning hours.

House rule

Teams is for school/class — not private chats with unknown people.
