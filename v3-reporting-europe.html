Report & Get Help — Europe

If a child may be at risk in Europe, protect the child first, preserve evidence, and use your country’s reporting pathway as quickly as possible.

Important Europe note

Europe does not use one single reporting body for every country.

The safest starting point is to find your own national hotline or official reporting route first.

Country-specific reporting matters

Main Europe reporting pathway

INHOPE helps people find their local hotline for reporting suspected child sexual abuse material online. It explains that trained analysts at national hotlines review reports and assess whether the material is illegal. 12

Find Your Hotline (INHOPE) How Reporting Works

What to do right away

  • Keep the child safe and stop further contact if safe to do so.
  • Tell the child they are not in trouble.
  • Save screenshots, usernames, profile links, and timestamps.
  • Write down what happened in simple notes.
  • Do not delete messages or accounts before preserving evidence.
You do not need to be certain before making a report. INHOPE says that if you encounter suspected CSAM online, you should report it, even if you are unsure. 13

How Europe reporting usually works

  • You find your national hotline through INHOPE.
  • The hotline reviews the report.
  • Illegal content can then be referred onward for action, including cooperation with law enforcement and takedown processes.
INHOPE explains that reports are reviewed by trained analysts and can help investigations, offender identification, and victim safeguarding. 14
