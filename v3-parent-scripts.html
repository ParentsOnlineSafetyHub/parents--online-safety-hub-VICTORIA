Golden Rule Script

NO OFF-PLATFORM CHATS

If anyone asks you to move from the game/app to Discord, Snapchat, Telegram, WhatsApp or Instagram DMs — that’s a RED FLAG.

You tell me immediately. You won’t be in trouble. I’ll protect you and handle it.

How to use these scripts

Pick one script and repeat it weekly (not during conflict). The goal is to build a child’s automatic response: “Tell a parent early.”

  • Say it calm (no interrogation).
  • Say it short (kids switch off with long speeches).
  • Say it often (repetition builds safety habits).
  • Follow through: no punishment for telling the truth.

Jump to age

Under 10 (simple + safety-first)

Goal: supervised play + no stranger contact.

Core Script:
“If someone talks to you online and you don’t know them in real life, you tell me straight away.”
  • Friends rule: “Friends online must be friends in real life. If we don’t know them, we remove them.”
  • Chat rule: “We don’t do private messages. We play, then we hop off.”
  • Voice rule: “No voice chat with strangers. If voice turns on, you tell me.”
  • Safety comfort: “You’re never in trouble for telling me. Ever.”

10–12 (habits + boundaries)

Goal: build trust habits before teen years.

Core Script:
“I’m not here to spy. I’m here to protect you. If anything feels weird online, you tell me and you won’t be punished.”
  • Weekly check: “Let’s do our 5-minute safety check together.”
  • DM boundary: “DMs are friends-only. Strangers don’t get private access.”
  • Off-platform rule: “If anyone says ‘add me on Discord/Snap/Telegram’, that’s a red flag. You show me.”
  • Secrets rule: “Good surprises are birthday surprises. Online secrets aren’t allowed.”

13–15 (guided independence)

Goal: independence with accountability.

Core Script:
“I trust you. My job is to protect you from adults who target kids. If someone tries to isolate you or push secrecy, we act fast.”
  • Respect + check: “I’ll respect privacy, but I won’t ignore safety. We do regular checks together.”
  • Red flags language: “Flattery, gifts, ‘you’re mature’, ‘don’t tell your parents’ — those are danger signs.”
  • Evidence script: “Screenshot it. Don’t argue. Block/report. Then show me.”
  • Off-platform escalation: “If they push to Discord/Telegram/Snap/WhatsApp, that’s escalation. You tell me immediately.”
If they confess something scary:
“Thank you for telling me. You’re safe. You’re not in trouble. We’re handling this together.”

16+ (trust + adult-level safety)

Goal: teach adult tactics + self-protection.

Core Script:
“I’m not trying to control you. I’m trying to keep you safe from manipulation, blackmail and exploitation. If anything feels off, I’m on your side.”
  • Consent + pressure: “If you feel pressured, that’s not consent. Pressure is a red flag.”
  • Blackmail line: “If anyone threatens you, stop contact and tell me immediately. We’ll report it.”
  • Meet-up boundary: “No meeting anyone from online without a trusted adult involved.”
  • Digital footprint: “Anything sent can be saved. Even ‘disappearing’ messages can be captured.”
Support script:
“Whatever happened, we’re dealing with it together. Safety first. Shame off.”

Situation scripts (copy/paste lines)

When you want to check their phone/console:
“We’re doing our safety check together. It’s not punishment — it’s protection.”
When they get defensive:
“I’m not accusing you. I’m blocking strangers from accessing you. Same team.”
When you find an unknown ‘friend’:
“We don’t know them in real life, so we remove them. If they’re safe, they won’t mind.”
When they admit they moved to Discord/Snap:
“Thank you for telling me. You’re not in trouble. We’ll block them and keep you safe.”
When you need the ‘no shame’ promise:
“You will never lose your device for telling the truth. Telling me is always the right move.”
