Parent Scripts

Say it calm. Say it often. Kids speak up when they feel safe.

Most Important Script

You will not be in trouble for telling me something that happened online.

My job is to protect you, not punish you. We handle it together.

Kids speak sooner when they do not fear punishment

How to use these scripts

Pick one or two lines and repeat them weekly. The goal is not a lecture — it is building a calm safety habit.

  • Keep it short.
  • Use the same words often.
  • Say it outside conflict, not only when there is a problem.
  • Follow through: no punishment for honesty.

Under 10

Use this:
“If someone you don’t know talks to you online, you tell me straight away.”
  • “We only talk to people we know in real life.”
  • “No private chats. No secrets.”
  • “If something feels weird, close it and get me.”

10–12

Use this:
“If anyone asks you to move to another app like Discord or Snapchat, that’s a red flag. You show me.”
  • “Online friends must be real-life friends.”
  • “Good surprises are birthdays. Online secrets are not okay.”
  • “You won’t lose your device for telling me the truth.”

13–15

Use this:
“Predators use compliments, secrecy, and moving chats off-platform. If anyone does that, I need to know early.”
  • “If anyone asks for photos, secrecy, or personal info — we block and report.”
  • “I will stay calm. You tell me; we handle it together.”
  • “Flattery, gifts, and ‘you’re mature for your age’ can be manipulation.”

16+

Use this:
“I trust you. My role is to protect you from manipulation, blackmail, and exploitation.”
  • “If anyone threatens you, stop contact and tell me immediately.”
  • “Pressure is a red flag. Real respect does not need secrecy.”
  • “Anything sent can be saved, even disappearing messages.”

When your child tells you something serious

Say this:
“Thank you for telling me. You did the right thing. You are not in trouble. We are going to keep you safe.”
