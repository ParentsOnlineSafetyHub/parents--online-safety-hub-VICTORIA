10 Signs Your Child May Be Targeted Online

Not every sign means danger. But when several signs appear together, parents should act early.

Why this page matters

Online grooming and manipulation rarely begin with something obvious.

They often start slowly — secrecy, private contact, emotional trust, or gifts.

Patterns matter more than one single moment

1. Sudden secrecy around devices

Hiding screens, clearing chats, turning devices away, switching apps quickly, or refusing normal checks may signal something is happening online.

2. A new online “friend” they will not explain

If your child talks frequently with someone online but becomes vague or defensive when asked about them, pay attention.

3. Mood changes after gaming or social apps

Sudden anxiety, shame, anger, sadness, or emotional crashes after being online can signal pressure or manipulation.

4. Repeated late-night device use

Hidden contact often grows late at night when children feel isolated and parents are less likely to notice.

5. Gifts, Robux, skins, money, or “help” from someone online

Gifts are commonly used to build trust or create a sense of loyalty and obligation.

6. Pressure to move to another app

Moving conversations from games or public spaces to Discord, Snapchat, Telegram, WhatsApp, or DMs increases risk significantly.

7. Talk of secrecy

Statements like “Don’t tell your parents” or “This stays between us” are major warning signs.

8. Emotional attachment to someone online

If your child becomes strongly attached to someone they have never met, manipulation may be occurring.

9. Requests for photos or personal details

Any request for photos, location, personal information, or more private contact should be treated seriously.

10. Your instincts say something feels wrong

Parents do not need perfect proof before acting. If something feels off, investigate calmly.

Your instinct is an early warning system.

What to do next

If several signs appear together, act calmly and clearly.
