Virtual Reality

VR is high exposure. Voice-first and immersive spaces can escalate contact quickly.

Why VR matters so much

VR puts children into live, immersive, voice-based environments where strangers can feel far more personal and believable.

This can speed up grooming, manipulation, and unsafe contact.

VR is one of the highest-exposure environments for children

VR settings to check now

1) Restrict voice chat where possible

2) Limit who can contact or invite the child

3) Review account privacy and friend settings

4) Keep use supervised where possible

5) Treat private worlds and rooms as high risk

Common VR games / spaces to check

VRChat Rec Room Gorilla Tag Meta / Horizon Style Spaces

What parents need to know about VR

  • Voice chat makes strangers feel more trusted, faster
  • Private rooms/worlds reduce visibility
  • Adults and children may mix in the same spaces
  • Children often forget how real-time and exposed they are in VR
