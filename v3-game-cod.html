Call of Duty

Voice chat and competitive multiplayer can expose children to strangers quickly.

Why parents should know Call of Duty

Call of Duty is fast, competitive, and highly social during live matches.

Open voice chat, party invites, and regular team play can make stranger contact feel normal very quickly.

Competitive voice chat can create fast access to children

Important settings

1) Disable open voice chat where possible

2) Restrict friend requests

3) Avoid public voice channels

4) Use party-only voice chat

5) Apply console or platform privacy settings first

Red flags

  • Players asking children to join Discord
  • Players inviting children to private chats
  • Players requesting social media
  • Players encouraging secrecy
  • Adults trying to build repeated contact after matches

What parents should do

1) Ask whether your child is using public chat or party-only chat

2) Watch for repeated invites from the same stranger

3) Set a clear rule: no moving game contact into private apps without parent knowledge

4) Keep younger children away from open voice channels where possible
