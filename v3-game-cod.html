Call of Duty

Competitive multiplayer = high contact. Lock chat and invites first.

Why COD is high exposure

COD has public matchmaking, voice chat, and fast contact with strangers. The biggest risk isn’t the gameplay — it’s open comms and random lobbies.

Safety locks (do these first)

1) Voice chat OFF (or friends-only).

2) Disable private messages from non-friends.

3) Restrict who can add/friend your child.

4) Turn off cross-play chat if you can’t supervise it.

5) Use time limits + breaks (reduces late-night risk behaviour).

Platform-first controls (strongest layer)

Parent check (30 seconds)

Once a week: open COD → check chat settings + recent friends list. If it’s hidden, it’s a problem.
