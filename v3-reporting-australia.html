Reporting Online Safety Concerns (Australia)

Act early. Stay calm. Report properly.

If a child is in immediate danger

Call 000 immediately

If a child is in immediate risk of harm or exploitation, contact emergency services first. Evidence can be gathered afterwards.

eSafety Commissioner

Australia’s national online safety regulator.

Use eSafety to report:

  • Cyberbullying involving children
  • Image-based abuse
  • Serious online harassment
  • Illegal or harmful content
Report to eSafety

ACCCE – Australian Federal Police

The Australian Centre to Counter Child Exploitation (ACCCE) handles reports related to online child sexual exploitation.

  • Online grooming
  • Child exploitation material
  • Suspicious online contact with children
  • Predatory behaviour online
Report to ACCCE

State Police

You can also report directly to your local police station.

This may be appropriate when:

  • You know the person involved
  • There are threats or coercion
  • Offline contact may occur

Evidence Collection

If something suspicious happens online:

  • Take screenshots
  • Record usernames
  • Note dates and times
  • Save links or server names

Do not confront the individual directly. Preserve the evidence and report through the correct channels.

What parents should remember

Most cases begin with small conversations that escalate slowly over time.

Acting early can prevent further harm.
