How to Reset Your Child’s Brain After Too Much Screen Time

Overstimulation can be reversed. You don’t need perfection — you need reset patterns.

What a “reset” actually means

After heavy screen use, the brain is often overstimulated, reward-driven, and reactive.

A reset helps bring the brain back to a calmer, more balanced state.

Simple reset steps

1. Remove stimulation — turn off screens completely
2. Lower noise — reduce TV, music, background input
3. Slow environment — calm space, no pressure
4. Gentle activity — walk, draw, sit outside
5. Let boredom happen — don’t rush to fix it
The reset happens when stimulation stops — not when it’s replaced.

What NOT to do

❌ Replace one screen with another
❌ Force activity too quickly
❌ Expect instant behaviour change
