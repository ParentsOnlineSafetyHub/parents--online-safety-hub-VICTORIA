Why POSH Exists

One standard. Same boundaries. Safer families.

The problem

Kids are online earlier than ever. Games, social apps, group chats, live streams and DMs are part of daily life. The risk is not just “screen time” — it’s access.

Predators, scammers, and harmful communities don’t need your child’s address to cause harm. They only need contact, privacy, and time.

The gap

Most parents weren’t trained for this. Many don’t use the platforms their kids use. Even when parents care deeply, the settings are confusing, buried, and constantly changing.

That leaves families stuck in one of two places:

• Over-control (panic, bans, fights)

• Under-control (hoping it’s fine)

POSH exists to give families a third option: calm structure.

What POSH is

POSH is a free, practical hub that helps parents lock down settings step-by-step — starting at the device level, then platform, then app.

We keep it simple:

1) Age-appropriate boundaries

2) Device controls first (biggest win)

3) Platform settings next

4) Then specific apps and games

5) Conversation tools to build trust

What POSH is not

• It’s not fear-based.

• It’s not about shaming parents.

• It’s not about banning the internet.

It’s about helping families stay connected while staying protected.

Why support helps

POSH is built to stay free and accessible. Keeping it updated takes time and tools: writing guides, maintaining downloads, updating pages when platforms change, and creating resources parents can trust.

If you choose to support this movement, it helps keep POSH running, improving, and expanding across Australia.
