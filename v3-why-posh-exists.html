Why this exists

Children are online earlier than ever. Games, social apps, group chats, livestreams, private messages, and algorithm-driven content are now part of everyday life.

The risk is not just screen time. The real issue is access, exposure, privacy, and how quickly unsafe contact can grow without parents realising.

The risk is not just time online — it is who and what gets access
Child Safety First:
Harm online does not always begin with something obvious. It often begins with access, secrecy, private contact, pressure, and repeated exposure.

The problem

Predators, scammers, manipulative communities, and harmful content systems do not need a child’s address to cause harm.

They often only need contact, privacy, time, and repeated access.

That access can happen through games, social apps, private chats, livestreams, creator influence, recommendation systems, or platforms that look harmless on the surface.

The gap

Most parents were never trained for this. Many did not grow up using the same platforms their children use now.

Even when parents care deeply, the settings are confusing, buried, inconsistent, and constantly changing.

That leaves families stuck in one of two places:

  • Over-control: panic, bans, conflict, shutdown
  • Under-control: hoping it is fine, trusting defaults, waiting too long
POSH exists to offer a third option: calm structure.

What POSH is

POSH is a practical child safety hub built to help parents reduce exposure step by step.

It gives families a simpler way to lock things down, understand the risks, and move toward safer online habits without panic.

What POSH is not

  • It is not fear-based.
  • It is not about shaming parents.
  • It is not about banning the internet.
  • It is not about pretending one talk fixes everything.
POSH is about helping families stay connected while staying protected.

Why support helps

POSH is built to stay simple, practical, and accessible. Keeping it useful takes ongoing work: updating guides, building downloads, expanding coverage, reviewing platform changes, and creating tools parents can actually use.

Support helps keep POSH running, improving, and reaching more families who need it.

The mission

To make online child safety clearer, calmer, and easier for parents to act on.

To reduce harm by helping families set safer defaults, stronger boundaries, and more open communication before problems escalate.
