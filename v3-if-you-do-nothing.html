If You Do Nothing

Most serious situations did not start serious. They became serious because early signs were missed or delayed.

This is how situations escalate

Online harm rarely begins with something obvious.

It usually starts small, looks harmless, and builds over time.

Delay is what allows escalation

The escalation path

Small contact
↓
Trust building
↓

↓
Secrecy
↓

↓

What “doing nothing” actually looks like

  • “It’s probably nothing”
  • “They’re just playing a game”
  • “It’s just an online friend”
  • “I don’t want to overreact”
  • “I’ll check later”
Most parents who faced serious situations said they did not realise how early it started.

What can happen if it escalates

  • Secrecy between child and parent increases
  • Emotional dependence on the other person
  • Pressure to keep conversations hidden
  • Requests for personal information or images
  • Threats, manipulation, or blackmail
By the time it feels serious, the situation is often already established.

What changes the outcome

Staying calm
