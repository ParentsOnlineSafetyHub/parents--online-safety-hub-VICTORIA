Report & Get Help — UK

If a child may be at risk in the United Kingdom, protect the child first, preserve evidence, and use the right reporting pathway quickly.

Immediate danger

If a child is in immediate danger, call 999 immediately.

If the concern involves online sexual abuse, grooming, or attempts to meet, preserve evidence and report it quickly.

Emergency first. Evidence second. Reporting next.

Main UK reporting pathways

CEOP: report concerns about grooming, sexual abuse, or someone trying to meet a child after online contact. 7

IWF: report suspected child sexual abuse imagery online. 8

Childline / Report Remove: support for under-18s trying to remove nude images shared online. 9

What to do right away

  • Keep the child safe and stop further contact if safe to do so.
  • Tell the child they are not in trouble.
  • Save screenshots, usernames, profile links, and timestamps.
  • Write down what happened in simple notes.
  • Do not delete messages or accounts before preserving evidence.
CEOP specifically advises parents to report concerns about grooming and sexual abuse online if something does not feel right. 10

When to use CEOP

  • Sexual communication with a child online
  • Online grooming or coercion
  • Someone trying to meet a child after online contact
  • Sexual pressure, threats, or exploitation

When to use IWF

Use IWF to report suspected child sexual abuse material online. INHOPE also points users to national hotlines like IWF for this purpose. 11
