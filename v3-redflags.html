Red Flags

Trust your instincts. Something feeling wrong is enough to act.

Do not wait for certainty

If something feels off, it matters.

You do not need proof before you start protecting your child.

Act early. Early action reduces harm.
Child Safety First: Red flags are not limited to one game or one app. Exposure can happen through gaming, social media, livestreams, private chats, algorithms, misinformation, and digital communities.

Behaviour changes to watch

  • Suddenly hiding screens when you walk in
  • Deleting chats or clearing history often
  • New online “friends” they won’t explain
  • Late-night device use
  • Emotional swings after gaming or messaging
  • Refusing simple safety checks
  • Multiple accounts or hidden profiles

Conversation red flags

  • “Don’t tell your parents.”
  • “Add me on Discord / Snap / Telegram.”
  • “You’re mature for your age.”
  • Requests for photos or private calls
  • Sudden gifts, game items, money, Nitro, skins
  • Pressure, guilt, or threats
Critical sign: moving a child from one platform to another is a major escalation point.

If your child tells you something

“Thank you for telling me. You’re not in trouble. We’re going to sort this out together.”

Children often stay silent because they fear losing their device or being blamed. Your first reaction matters.
