Snapchat

Snapchat increases privacy fast. Disappearing messages and private contact make it high risk for children.

Why Snapchat matters

Snapchat is built around private contact, disappearing messages, streaks, and image sharing.

That makes it one of the most common apps predators try to move children onto.

Disappearing messages make early warning signs easier to miss

Important Snapchat settings

1) Set contact settings so only known friends can contact the account

2) Turn off or restrict Quick Add

3) Turn off Snap Map / location sharing

4) Review friend list regularly

5) Teach your child not to use private snaps to hide contact

Major red flags on Snapchat

  • Unknown people added through Quick Add
  • Pressure to keep a streak going with strangers
  • Requests for photos or disappearing images
  • Messages that vanish before parents can see them
  • Someone saying “don’t tell your parents”
